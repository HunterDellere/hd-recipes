Equipment

Dutch Oven

5–7 quart enameled cast iron pot with a heavy lid. Mass for steady braising, seal for moisture, depth for stocks — and stovetop-to-oven without a transfer.

About

An enameled cast iron pot, 5–7 quart, with a heavy lid. The mass holds steady temperature for braising, the seal traps steam for moist cooking, and the depth handles stocks and stews without splashing. The defining feature is that it's stovetop-to-oven capable: a braise can sear on the stove and finish at 150°C in the oven without transferring to a second vessel, which preserves the fond and saves the dish from a temperature-drop step that pulls juices out of the meat.

Spec that matters: 5.5qt is the sweet spot for 4-person braises — large enough for a 1.5kg shoulder with aromatics, small enough that the liquid doesn't spread thin. Weight runs 5–8kg; the heft is the trade for the thermal stability. Enamel interior means you can deglaze with wine or tomato without worrying about reactive metal.

Substitutions

Workable alternatives when this tool is unavailable. The use case sets the bar each swap has to clear.

  • dutch oven → heavy stainless pot with tight lid About 80% there. Loses the residual-heat ramp-down that finishes a braise (stainless cools faster), and the oven-safe range above ~200°C if handles or knobs are plastic. Fine for low-and-slow stews; not for oven-finished braises above 200°C.
