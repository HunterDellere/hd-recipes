Equipment

Microplane

Photo-etched fine grater. Turns hard cheese, citrus zest, garlic, and ginger into a fluffy, dissolvable powder.

About

The geometry is the point. The teeth are stamped, not punched, so the cutting edges shave rather than tear. The output dissolves on contact with heat or acid in a way a box grater's output cannot — which is why a cacio e pepe sauce built with a microplane emulsifies smoothly while one built with coarse grate goes grainy.

Spec that matters: the "fine" or "zester" model, ~21cm cutting surface. Coarser variants exist; they're less useful for cheese, citrus zest, garlic, and ginger — the cases where the dissolving texture is the entire point.

Substitutions

Workable alternatives when this tool is unavailable. The use case sets the bar each swap has to clear.

  • microplane (cheese) → box grater, fine side About 70% of the way there. Strands still need real heat to integrate and they grain easily.
  • microplane (citrus zest) → paring knife Pull strips, mince fine. Slower, but the citrus oils stay intact longer because less surface area is exposed.
  • microplane (garlic to paste) → knife and salt Drag across the board, produces a workable smear.
