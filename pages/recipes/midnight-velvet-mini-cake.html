Midnight velvet mini cake with strawberry and balsamic caviar

A two-layer 4-inch chocolate cake bloomed with hot coffee, filled with strawberry reduction, finished with strawberry meringue buttercream, balsamic pearls, and tempered chocolate shards.

2 servings180 min total90 min activeharddessert
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Strawberry reduction

  1. 225 g fresh or frozen strawberries — (1.5 cups)
  2. 15 g fresh lemon juice — (1 tbsp)
  3. 15 g honey or maple syrupoptional — (1 tbsp)

Balsamic caviar

  1. 80 g strawberry reduction (from above) — (⅓ cup)
  2. 15 g aged balsamic vinegar — (1 tbsp)
  3. 15 g water — (1 tbsp)
  4. 1.5 g agar agar powder — (½ tsp; not flakes)
  5. 240 ml neutral oil, frozen 45+ minutes — (1 cup)

Tempered chocolate shards

  1. 115 g dark chocolate (60–70% cacao) — (4 oz; 3 oz for melting, 1 oz finely chopped to seed)

Cocoa sponge — bloom

  1. 25 g Dutch-process cocoa powder — (¼ cup)
  2. 60 ml hot brewed coffee — (¼ cup, near boiling)

Cocoa sponge — wet

  1. 100 g granulated sugar — (½ cup)
  2. 60 ml buttermilk (or kefir) — (¼ cup)
  3. 30 ml avocado oil — (2 tbsp)
  4. 1 each large egg, room temp
  5. 2.5 g vanilla extract — (½ tsp)

Cocoa sponge — dry

  1. 65 g all-purpose flour — (½ cup)
  2. 3 g baking soda — (½ tsp)
  3. 1 g baking powder — (¼ tsp)
  4. 1.5 g sea salt — (¼ tsp)

Strawberry meringue buttercream

  1. 2 each large egg whites
  2. 100 g granulated sugar — (½ cup)
  3. 170 g unsalted butter, cubed and cool — (¾ cup)
  4. 25 g freeze-dried strawberries, pulverized — (1 cup whole, ground to powder)
  5. 0.5 g sea salt — (pinch)

Execution

  1. 1
    Strawberry reduction: combine berries, lemon juice, and honey in a small saucepan. Cook over medium 10–12 minutes until the berries break down and the liquid thickens. Strain through a fine sieve, pressing hard on the solids. Set aside ⅓ cup for the caviar; reserve the rest for filling.15 min
  2. 2
    Balsamic caviar: confirm your tall glass of oil has been in the freezer at least 45 minutes. In a clean saucepan, whisk the ⅓ cup reduction with balsamic, water, and agar. Bring to a boil, simmer 2 minutes to activate the agar.8 min
  3. 3
    Pull off heat, cool briefly to ~125°F (50°C). Using a pipette, drop droplets into the cold oil — they'll set into pearls as they fall. Strain the pearls out, rinse gently with cold water. Refrigerate.10 min
  4. 4
    Tempered chocolate shards: melt the 3 oz portion of chocolate in a bowl (microwave bursts or double boiler) to 115°F (46°C). Pull off heat, immediately add the 1 oz finely chopped seed chocolate, and stir constantly.8 min
  5. 5
    Continue stirring until the chocolate cools to 82°F (27°C). Briefly reheat (3–5 second microwave bursts) back to 88–90°F (31–32°C) — do not exceed 91°F. Spread thinly on parchment, let set at room temp until matte, then refrigerate 10 minutes. Snap into shards.20 min
  6. 6
    Sponge: heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease two 4-inch round pans. Whisk the cocoa with hot coffee in a large bowl until it forms a dark glossy paste. Let sit 2 minutes — the heat melts the cocoa butter and hydrates the starch, opening the flavor.5 min
  7. 7
    Whisk sugar and buttermilk into the hot cocoa paste. The cold buttermilk drops the temperature so the egg won't scramble. Whisk in oil, vanilla, and the egg until smooth.2 min
  8. 8
    In a small bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Dump dry into the wet bowl and whisk just until no streaks remain.2 min
  9. 9
    Divide between the two pans. Bake 22–25 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool completely before assembling.25 min
  10. 10
    Buttercream: whisk egg whites and sugar over a double boiler until they hit 160°F (71°C) and the sugar dissolves.5 min
  11. 11
    Transfer to a stand mixer; whip on high to stiff peaks, until the bowl is cool to the touch. Add butter cubes one at a time on low speed; whip until silky. Fold in the freeze-dried strawberry powder and a pinch of salt.8 min
  12. 12
    Assemble: level the cakes. Pipe a dam of buttercream around the edge of the bottom layer. Fill the center with the reserved strawberry reduction. Top with the second layer.5 min
  13. 13
    Coat the outside in a full layer of buttercream and smooth the sides. Spoon the balsamic caviar onto the center. Press the chocolate shards into the cake at varying angles for height.5 min

Substitutions

  • plain batter → + ⅓ cup toasted chopped hazelnuts Ferrero-Rocher-adjacent flavor that pairs with the strawberry.
  • butter and egg-white components → plant-based butter, aquafaba, flax egg in cake Vegan version. Aquafaba whips like egg whites for the buttercream.
  • plain reduction → + pinch of black pepper Pepper enhances strawberry sweetness and bridges to the balsamic.

Notes

The "true bloom" — mixing cocoa with hot coffee before flour or eggs — does what dry-blending the cocoa with flour can''t. Heat melts the cocoa butter and hydrates the starch granules, releasing flavor compounds that would otherwise stay locked away. The cake comes out a shade of brown most all-cocoa cakes never reach.

Tempering is about cocoa butter crystals. Cocoa butter is polymorphic — it can solidify in several crystal forms, only one of which (Form V) is shiny, hard, and snappy. Melting fully (115°F) wipes the slate. Cooling with seed chocolate (82°F) seeds Form V. Reheating slightly (90°F) melts any inferior crystals while leaving Form V intact. Skip the temper and the chocolate goes dull and bendy at room temp.

The balsamic caviar is straight cold-oil spherification. Agar sets when it cools below ~40°C, and the frozen oil drops the temperature instantly when each droplet enters. Drop too high and the pearls develop tails; drop too close and they merge.

The reduction is doing more than flavor work for the filling — by boiling out the free water, you get a thick, pectin-rich jam that doesn''t make the cake soggy.
