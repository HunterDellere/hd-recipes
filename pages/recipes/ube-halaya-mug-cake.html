Ube halaya mug cake

A single-serving violet sponge whisked in the mug it bakes in and cooked in the microwave in about 90 seconds. Ube halaya carries the flavor, color, fat, and part of the sweetness all at once, so the rest is just flour, an egg, oil, and a pinch of leavener. No mixer, no oven, no second bowl, from craving to spoon in under five minutes.

Before you start

Read this first — the long-lead and coordination steps that need to be moving before mise.

  • Use a microwave-safe mug of at least 350 ml / 12 oz. The batter rises hard and fast in the microwave and a small mug overflows onto the turntable.
  • Bring the egg to room temperature if you can (a few minutes in warm water). A fridge-cold egg makes the batter sluggish and the center can stay raw while the edges overcook, though warm is a nicety here, not a requirement.

Execution

  1. 1
    In a microwave-safe mug of at least 350 ml / 12 oz, whisk the 60 g / 3 tbsp ube halaya, the 1 egg, the 15 g / 1 tbsp neutral oil, the 15 g / 1 tbsp whole milk, the 2 g / ½ tsp vanilla bean paste, and the optional 2 ml / ¼ tsp ube extract with a fork until completely smooth and uniform violet. Make sure no streaks of unmixed egg remain; they bake into tough spots.
  2. 2
    Add the 35 g / ¼ cup + 1 tbsp all-purpose flour, the 2 g / ½ tsp baking powder, and the pinch of fine sea salt directly into the mug. Whisk with the fork just until the batter is smooth and no dry flour remains, scraping the bottom corners of the mug where flour hides. Do not overbeat past smooth; a little restraint keeps the crumb tender. The batter should fill the mug no more than halfway, it will rise to the rim as it cooks.
  3. 3
    Microwave on full power for 80 to 100 seconds (1000 W oven; add 10 to 20 seconds for a 700 to 800 W oven). The cake rises dramatically, then settles slightly. It is done when the top looks set and springs back to a light touch and a toothpick comes out with just a moist crumb. Start at 80 seconds and add 10-second bursts if the center is still wet; stop the instant it sets, since 15 extra seconds turns a moist mug cake dry and bouncy.
  4. 4
    Let it stand 1 minute (the center finishes cooking from residual heat), then eat straight from the mug. An extra spoonful of halaya, a scoop of vanilla ice cream, or a splash of cream on top doubles down on the flavor while the cake is still warm.

Modifications

Pivots that change the dish's character — protein swaps, regional variants, dietary recastings.

  • Occasion molten center→ube halaya lava mug cake

    Whisk the batter as written, pour half into the mug, drop a 15 g / 1 tsp cold spoonful of ube halaya into the center, then top with the remaining batter so the spoonful is buried. Microwave the same time. The buried halaya stays soft and molten against the set sponge for a lava-cake center.

  • Texture richer, chocolate edge→ube-chocolate mug cake

    Drop 15 g / 1 tbsp chopped dark chocolate or chocolate chips into the batter before microwaving. The chocolate melts into pockets and the bitter edge plays against the sweet ube, the mug-cake version of the ube dark chocolate pairing.

  • Dietary eggless→no-egg version

    Skip the egg, increase the milk to 45 g / 3 tbsp, and add 10 g / 2 tsp more oil. The crumb is a touch denser and more tender-crumbly without the egg's structure, but it works for an egg-free single serving. Microwave the same time, checking 10 seconds early since the eggless batter sets faster.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • ube halaya → mashed cooked purple sweet potato + sweetened condensed milk (2:1 by weight) + 5 ml ube extract For the cook without Filipino groceries: rice cooked Stokes Purple or Okinawan purple sweet potato, weigh 40 g, and combine with 20 g sweetened condensed milk and 5 ml ube extract. The extract is needed here for both color and the recognizable ube flavor.
  • neutral oil → melted butter or melted coconut oil Melted butter adds flavor but firms the crumb more as it cools, so eat it hot if you swap. Coconut oil leans into the tropical register and pairs naturally with the halaya's coconut-milk base.
  • all-purpose flour → cake flour or a 1-to-1 gluten-free blend Cake flour makes an even more tender mug cake. A good 1-to-1 gluten-free blend (one that includes xanthan) works directly for a gluten-free version; the texture is slightly more delicate.

Make it from scratch

Store-bought is fine, but each of these has a homemade version that earns the time.

  • → ube halaya make it yourselfstub Homemade halaya has a brighter fresh-yam character than the jarred product, and since the halaya is essentially the entire flavor of this minimal cake, that brightness reads straight through. A jar of homemade kept in the fridge turns this into a 4-minute dessert any night.

Notes

Why halaya makes this so fast. A normal cake balances flour, sugar, fat, eggs, dairy, flavoring, and color from separate ingredients, which is why it needs a mixer, multiple bowls, and an oven. Ube halaya collapses four of those into one spoonable ingredient: it brings the violet color, the sweet yam-vanilla flavor, the dairy and butter fat, and a dose of cooked-yam starch all at once. That is why this batter needs almost nothing added, just enough flour and leavener for structure and lift, an egg to bind, and a little oil and milk for moisture, and why it comes together with a fork in one mug.

Why oil, not butter. Butter sets firm as it cools, which is fine for a cake you eat warm from the oven but works against a mug cake that you might eat over a few minutes. Oil stays liquid at room temperature, so the crumb reads moist and soft even as the mug cools on the desk. If you only have butter, use it but eat the cake hot.

The overcook cliff. The single thing that ruins a mug cake is fifteen extra seconds. The microwave drives water out of a small batter fast, and the difference between a moist, tender cake and a dry, bouncy, rubbery one is a narrow window right at the end. Start low (80 seconds), check, and add time in 10-second bursts only if the center is still wet. Pull it the instant the top sets and springs back; the standing minute afterward finishes the center with residual heat without drying the crumb.

Color holds better in the microwave than the oven. Ube''s anthocyanin fades with sustained heat above 80 °C, which is why oven bakes lose 15 to 20% of their violet. A mug cake cooks in 90 seconds, so the pigment has far less time to degrade and the natural halaya color reads brighter out of the microwave than out of an oven. The optional ube extract is genuinely optional here, more so than in any of the oven bakes; add the few drops only if you want maximum violet.
