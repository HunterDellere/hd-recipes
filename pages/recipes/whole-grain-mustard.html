Whole-grain mustard

Same Dijon chemistry, but pulsed coarse — the seeds stay intact and pop between the teeth, releasing heat in waves rather than all at once.

Before you start

Read this first — the long-lead and coordination steps that need to be moving before mise.

  • The seeds need a 36 to 48 hour cold soak in wine and vinegar BEFORE the food processor work. Build the soak two days ahead of when you want to use the mustard; rushing this step produces seeds that crunch hard rather than pop cleanly and a mustard that reads raw and astringent.
  • After grinding, the mustard mellows over another 48 hours in the refrigerator and stabilizes by day five. Plan the full 4 to 5 day calendar; mustard tasted on day one reads aggressively hot and the bite softens predictably over the rest.
  • Use only glass, ceramic, or stainless equipment at every stage. Mustard reacts with aluminum and untreated cast iron within hours and develops a metallic edge that cannot be reversed.
  • Pulse the food processor in 1 second bursts, never run continuously. A continuous run produces smooth Dijon in 30 seconds; the structural goal here is roughly half-cracked, half-intact seeds, which the pulse-and-pause action lets you watch and stop at.

Soak

  1. 60 g brown mustard seeds
  2. 30 g yellow mustard seeds
  3. 150 ml dry white wine, Sauvignon Blanc or unoaked Chardonnay
  4. 60 ml apple cider vinegar

Finish

  1. 6 g kosher salt
  2. 12 g honey optional

Execution

  1. 1
    Combine both mustard seeds, wine, and vinegar in a glass or ceramic bowl. Cover and refrigerate 36–48 hours. The seeds will swell and soften but should still feel firm in the center; for whole-grain you want the pop, not full softness.
  2. 2
    After the soak, transfer to a food processor. Add 6 g / 1 tsp kosher salt and 12 g / 2 tsp honey.
  3. 3
    Pulse (emphatically pulse, do not run continuously) in 1-second bursts, 8 to 12 times. The goal is to crush half the seeds while leaving the other half intact. Stop when the mixture has a thick, scoopable texture and roughly half the seeds are still whole.
  4. 4
    Taste. The bite should be sharp but the texture coarse: each bite delivers a different mix of cracked-seed heat and intact-seed pop. If too thick, splash in a tablespoon of cold water and pulse twice more.
  5. 5
    Transfer to a clean glass jar, press plastic wrap onto the surface, seal, and refrigerate. The bite mellows over 48 hours and stabilizes by day five.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • apple cider vinegar → white wine vinegar Sharper, more austere: the version that reads as Pommery. Cider is rounder and more American.
  • honey → maple syrup Different aromatic base; works particularly well in glaze applications.

Notes

The structural difference vs smooth Dijon: same chemistry, same heat release from myrosinase + glucosinolates, but the texture changes everything. Whole seeds pop one or two at a time as you chew, releasing heat in waves; smooth Dijon delivers it all at once. The two mustards behave completely differently in a vinaigrette, on a charcuterie board, or in a glaze.

Why pulse not blend: a high-speed blender will turn this into smooth Dijon in about 30 seconds. The food processor's pulse-and-pause action lets you watch the breakdown and stop when you have the right ratio of cracked to intact. Aim for chunky like couscous, not fine like sand.

Why the soak liquid is mostly wine + vinegar (not water + vinegar like smooth Dijon): the seeds need to absorb less liquid for the coarse cut to hold its shape. Whole-grain mustard with too much water is sloppy and runs off the meat.

Why metal-free: same as smooth Dijon. Mustard reacts with reactive metals (aluminum, untreated cast iron) and develops a metallic edge within hours. Glass, ceramic, or stainless only.

Pairs hard with: maple-glazed pork or turkey, pretzel dips, vinaigrettes for grain salads, raclette night, spread on a ham and gruyère sandwich.

Storage: airtight in the fridge, 6 months. Improves over the first month as the heat rounds out and the texture softens.
