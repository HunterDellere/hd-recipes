Homemade crystal boba (agar pearls)

Clear, jelly-bright pearls made from agar-agar dropped through cold oil into a chilled bath. A different texture entirely from tapioca boba, snappy and refreshing rather than chewy and dense. Built for fruit teas where tapioca would clash.

Before you start

Read this first — the long-lead and coordination steps that need to be moving before mise.

  • Chill the oil bath first, 4 hours in the refrigerator or 1 hour in the freezer. The technique depends on cold oil; room-temperature or warm oil will not produce round pearls. Do this the day before if possible.
  • Crystal boba holds 5 days refrigerated in syrup, unlike tapioca which has a 4-hour window. Make these whenever and use throughout the week.

Mise en place by phase 3 phases ▾

Cold oil bath (prepared first)

  1. 500 ml neutral oil (vegetable, canola, or grapeseed)

Agar solution

  1. 240 ml water
  2. 6 g agar-agar powder
  3. 30 g granulated sugar

Holding syrup

  1. 240 ml water
  2. 100 g granulated sugar

Execution

  1. 1
    Prepare the cold oil bath the day before. Pour the 500 ml / 2 cups neutral oil into a tall narrow glass or jar, taller than wide is important so the agar droplets have enough fall distance to form spherical before hitting the bottom. Refrigerate at least 4 hours (or freeze 1 hour). The oil should be visibly cold and slightly viscous on the spoon. Cold oil is the entire technique, agar drops falling through warm oil flatten and fail to gel into rounds.
  2. 2
    Combine the 240 ml / 1 cup water and the 6 g / 1½ tsp agar-agar powder in a small saucepan. Whisk thoroughly off-heat to break up any clumps. Let stand 2 minutes for the agar to bloom in the cold water.
  3. 3
    Bring the agar mixture to a hard rolling boil over medium-high heat, whisking constantly. Hold at the rolling boil for 90 seconds, agar requires full boil for full activation; sub-boiled agar sets weak and grainy. Whisk in the 30 g / 2½ tbsp sugar; boil 30 more seconds to dissolve.
  4. 4
    Pull off heat. Let the agar solution cool for 1 to 2 minutes, it needs to drop below 80°C for the oil-drop method to work but stay above 40°C or it will start gelling in the dropper. Aim for a temperature where the liquid is hot but pourable, with no skin forming yet.
  5. 5
    Pull the cold oil from the refrigerator. Using a small dropper, pipette, or fine-tipped squeeze bottle, drop the warm agar solution into the cold oil one drop at a time from about 3 cm / 1¼ inches above the oil surface. Each drop forms a perfect sphere as it falls through the cold oil and the agar gels on contact with the cold. The pearls sink to the bottom. Work fast, you have about 5 minutes before the agar in the dropper sets and clogs. Re-warm gently if it does.
  6. 6
    When all pearls are dropped, let them rest in the cold oil for 5 minutes to fully set. Pour the oil through a fine sieve to catch the pearls. Rinse the pearls thoroughly under cold running water for 30 seconds, working them gently with your fingers to wash off all surface oil. They should feel clean and squeaky, not slick.
  7. 7
    In a small saucepan, combine the 240 ml / 1 cup water and 100 g / ½ cup sugar for the holding syrup. Bring to a simmer, stir to dissolve, then cool to room temperature. Transfer the rinsed pearls into the cool syrup. The syrup both flavors and keeps the pearls from drying out.
  8. 8
    Refrigerate the pearls in the syrup. Unlike tapioca boba, crystal boba holds beautifully in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, the agar gel is stable on cooling rather than retrograding. Use as needed.

Modifications

Pivots that change the dish's character — protein swaps, regional variants, dietary recastings.

  • Occasion flavor and color→lychee crystal boba

    Replace the 240 ml water in the agar solution with 240 ml lychee juice (canned syrup works). Add a tiny pinch of citric acid to balance. Produces pale-pink scented pearls excellent in jasmine fruit teas.

  • Occasion flavor and color→butterfly pea blue crystal boba

    Steep 1 tbsp dried butterfly pea flowers in the 240 ml hot water for 10 minutes; strain and use the blue water in the agar solution. The pearls turn vivid sapphire blue with no synthetic dye. Adding a few drops of lime juice to the holding syrup shifts the color to purple-pink.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • agar-agar powder → agar flakes, doubled (12 g / 3 tbsp) Flakes are roughly half the strength of powder by weight. Soak in the cold water for 10 minutes before the boil, flakes hydrate slower than powder.

Notes

Cold oil is the entire technique. Warm or even room-temperature oil does not work, the agar droplets do not gel fast enough on contact and they distort or flatten as they fall. The colder the oil, the cleaner the spheres. Some practitioners freeze the oil briefly until it just starts to thicken, which gives the most perfect spheres at the cost of slightly slower work.

Agar vs. gelatin for crystal boba. Gelatin melts at body temperature and would dissolve in any warm drink. Agar holds shape up to 85°C, which means crystal boba survives even in hot teas. The thermal stability is why agar is the right hydrocolloid for this application.

These are not interchangeable with tapioca boba. Crystal boba reads snappy, juicy, and clean; tapioca reads chewy, dense, and starchy. Use crystal pearls in fruit-forward drinks (lychee, peach, passion fruit) where the lighter texture complements the sweetness; use tapioca in milk-forward and brown-sugar drinks where the chewy density anchors the dairy.
