Recipe

Chai pumpkin cupcakes

Pumpkin cupcakes with the spice infused into the milk like a real chai, then frosted with brown butter cream cheese.

12 servings90 min total40 min activemediumdessert
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Chai milk

  1. 120 ml whole milk — (½ cup; you'll lose some to evaporation)
  2. 4 g ground Ceylon cinnamon — (2 tsp)
  3. 3 g ground ginger — (1½ tsp)
  4. 1 g ground cardamom — (½ tsp)
  5. 1 g ground allspice — (½ tsp)
  6. 0.5 g ground cloves — (¼ tsp)
  7. 1 each fresh gingeroptional — ½-inch knob, smashed
  8. 6 g Assam tea, loose leaf — (2 tbsp; or 3 strong tea bags)

Wet

  1. 240 g pumpkin puree — (1 cup; pure pumpkin, not pie filling)
  2. 110 g avocado oil or light olive oil — (½ cup)
  3. 2 each large eggs, room temp
  4. 110 g dark brown sugar, packed — (½ cup)
  5. 80 g maple syrup — (⅓ cup)
  6. 5 g vanilla extract — (1 tsp)

Dry

  1. 190 g all-purpose flour — (1½ cups)
  2. 4 g baking powder — (1 tsp)
  3. 3 g baking soda — (½ tsp)
  4. 3 g fine sea salt — (½ tsp)

Frosting

  1. 115 g unsalted butter — (½ cup / 1 stick)
  2. 225 g cream cheese, brick-style, cold — (8 oz, full-fat)
  3. 180 g powdered sugar, sifted — (1½ cups)
  4. 5 g vanilla bean paste — (1 tsp)

Execution

  1. 1
    Combine milk and all ground spices in a small saucepan with the smashed ginger. Bring to a low simmer and hold there 3–5 minutes; the spices need active heat to bloom.5 min
  2. 2
    Pull from heat. Drop in the Assam tea, cover, and steep exactly 5 minutes. Strain through a fine sieve, pressing hard on the solids. You want about 90 ml (⅓ cup + 2 tbsp). Cool fully.8 min
  3. 3
    In a small skillet over medium, cook the pumpkin puree, stirring often, for 5–7 minutes. The color shifts from bright orange to a deeper rust as the water cooks off. Cool slightly.7 min
  4. 4
    Heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-cup muffin tin.
  5. 5
    Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl.1 min
  6. 6
    In a large bowl, whisk the cooled roasted pumpkin, oil, eggs, brown sugar, maple syrup, vanilla, and the cooled chai milk until smooth.2 min
  7. 7
    Pour wet into dry. Switch to a spatula and fold gently until no flour streaks remain. The batter can be a touch lumpy.2 min
  8. 8
    Divide between liners. Bake 18–22 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean and the tops spring back. Cool completely on a rack.22 min
  9. 9
    For the frosting, brown the butter in a saucepan until amber and nutty. Pour into a bowl and refrigerate ~45 minutes until it firms back to the texture of soft butter. Brown butter (beurre noisette)50 min
  10. 10
    Whip the solidified brown butter with the cold cream cheese until creamy. Add powdered sugar and vanilla. Whip until fluffy. Frost the cooled cupcakes.4 min

Substitutions

  • all-purpose flour → 1:1 gluten-free blend King Arthur Measure for Measure works. The roasted pumpkin gives enough structure for GF.
  • cardamom + cinnamon → ¼ tsp cardamom + 2½ tsp cinnamon For a less spice-forward, more pumpkin-spice-latte profile.

Notes

Why the milk gets simmered but the tea gets steeped off-heat: spice cell walls are tough and need active heat to release their oils. Tea leaves are delicate — boil them and you pull bitter tannins. Two phases, one infusion, no astringency.

Pan-roasting canned pumpkin drives off excess water and starts a light Maillard. The result is a deeper, sweeter base that doesn''t taste raw or metallic against the chai spice.

Browning the butter before whipping the frosting evaporates water (so the frosting stays stable on the cupcake) and toasts the milk solids — the nutty notes bridge the spice and the cream cheese tang.
