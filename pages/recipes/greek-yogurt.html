Strained Greek yogurt

Whole-milk yogurt cultured at 42 to 45 °C, then strained 4 to 8 hours through cheesecloth to concentrate the protein and remove the whey. The cooked-in-cheesecloth-bag method versus the stand-mixer-bowl-and-sieve method. Stub.

Mise en Place

Servings
Units
Shopping list 3 items ▾

Dairy, eggs & refrigerated 3

  1. 100 ml heavy cream optional
  2. 60 g plain whole-milk yogurt with live active cultures (as starter)
  3. 2000 ml whole milk (full-fat, 3.5 to 4 percent butterfat; non-ultra-pasteurized for best texture)

Execution

  1. 1
    Stub: heat the 2 L whole milk to 82 °C / 180 °F in a heavy pot, holding for 5 minutes. The proteins denature and the resulting yogurt sets firmer; ultra-pasteurized milk has been heated past this point in processing already and produces a looser yogurt.
  2. 2
    Stub: cool the milk to 43 °C / 110 °F (the temperature window where yogurt cultures are active without being killed by heat).
  3. 3
    Stub: whisk the 60 g starter yogurt into a small portion of the warm milk to disperse, then whisk back into the rest. Pour into clean glass jars, cover, and incubate at 43 °C for 6 to 8 hours (a yogurt-maker, an oven with the light on, or a thermos works). Set yogurt should jiggle as a unit when the jar is tilted.8 h
  4. 4
    Stub: refrigerate the set yogurt 2 hours to firm. Then strain through cheesecloth or a fine fabric for 4 to 8 hours; longer strain produces thicker, labneh-like consistency. Reserve the whey (rich in protein, useful in baking and smoothies).6 h

Notes

This is a stub. Flesh out: the milk-protein-denaturation chemistry (why heating to 82 °C produces firmer set), the lactic-acid bacteria (Lactobacillus bulgaricus + Streptococcus thermophilus, the canonical Greek strains), the temperature window for incubation (above 46 °C kills the cultures, below 38 °C the cure runs slow and the texture is loose), the strain-time-to-texture chart (4 hours = standard Greek yogurt, 8 hours = labneh-thick, 12 hours = labneh-cream-cheese), the salt question (some labneh recipes salt before straining; pure Greek yogurt is unsalted), the storage chemistry (live cultures continue to acidify in the fridge; use within 10 days for best flavor balance, or it turns sharply sour), and the troubleshooting tree (broken yogurt, ropy yogurt, stringy yogurt — each has a specific cause).
