Double dark chocolate crinkle cookies

Fudgy crinkle cookies built on melted dark chocolate, bloomed cocoa, and a coconut-oil tweak that keeps them glossy and chewy.

16 servings26 min total20 min activemediumdessert
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Chocolate base

  1. 60 g unsalted butter — (2 oz / 4 tbsp)
  2. 28 g coconut oil — (2 tbsp; refined for neutral flavor)
  3. 115 g dark chocolate, chopped — (4 oz, 70% cacao)
  4. 50 g Dutch-process cocoa powder — (½ cup)

Wet

  1. 2 each large eggs
  2. 150 g granulated sugar — (¾ cup)
  3. 110 g light brown sugar — (½ cup, packed)
  4. 5 g vanilla bean paste — (1 tsp)
  5. 3 g espresso powder — (1 tsp)

Dry

  1. 130 g all-purpose flour — (1 cup + 1 tbsp)
  2. 4 g baking powder — (1 tsp)
  3. 3 g kosher salt — (½ tsp)

Mix-ins & coating

  1. 145 g dark or semi-sweet chocolate chunks — (5 oz, hand-chopped from a bar)
  2. 50 g granulated sugar, for rolling — (¼ cup)
  3. 60 g powdered sugar, for rolling — (½ cup)

Execution

  1. 1
    Melt butter, coconut oil, and chopped chocolate together (microwave in 20-second bursts or set over a pot of simmering water). Whisk in the cocoa while the fat is still hot — the heat hydrates the cocoa and pulls out more flavor than mixing it in dry.5 min
  2. 2
    In a stand mixer, whip eggs, granulated sugar, and brown sugar on medium-high for 4 minutes until pale and ribbony. Whisk in vanilla and espresso powder.5 min
  3. 3
    Pour the cooled chocolate mix into the eggs and mix on low until combined. Add flour, baking powder, and salt; fold until almost combined.2 min
  4. 4
    Fold in the chocolate chunks. Cover and chill at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. The coconut oil makes the dough soft, so this chill is what stops the cookies from spreading flat.240 min
  5. 5
    Heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Scoop 1.5-tbsp balls. Roll lightly in granulated sugar, then heavily in powdered sugar. The granulated layer keeps the powdered sugar from melting on contact and giving you a smooth shell.5 min
  6. 6
    Bake 10–11 minutes. The edges should set; the centers should still look soft. The chunks inside stay molten as the cookie cools.11 min

Notes

Use chopped bar chocolate, not chips. Chips contain stabilizers that hold their shape — fine for chocolate-chip cookies, wrong here. Bar chocolate melts into actual puddles, leaving strata of chocolate in the cookie.

The double dredge is what makes the crinkle. The granulated layer is a moisture barrier; the powdered sugar above it stays white and snowy as the cookie spreads, opening into the cracked, contrast-y top.
