British mushy peas

Marrowfat peas soaked overnight with bicarb, simmered to soft, then crushed into a coarse green puree seasoned with butter, salt, and white pepper. The canonical green side for fish and chips. Stub.

Mise en Place

Servings
Units
Shopping list 6 items ▾
  1. 200 g dried marrowfat peas (UK Batchelors or similar), soaked overnight in 800 ml water with 5 g bicarbonate of soda (baking soda)
  2. 800 ml water (for the soak)
  3. 5 g bicarbonate of soda (baking soda)
  4. 30 g unsalted butter
  5. 6 g kosher salt
  6. 1 g white pepper

Execution

  1. 1
    Stub: combine the 200 g marrowfat peas with the 800 ml water and 5 g bicarb in a bowl; soak overnight (12+ hours). The bicarb softens the pea skins.
  2. 2
    Stub: drain and rinse the peas. Cover with fresh water by 5 cm in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer, partially covered, and cook 45 to 60 minutes until completely soft and beginning to fall apart on their own.
  3. 3
    Stub: drain (reserve a little cooking liquid). Mash with a fork or potato masher to a coarse green puree (not a smooth puree; the texture should have visible whole peas mixed with mashed). Stir in the 30 g butter, 6 g salt, 1 g white pepper. Adjust with a splash of cooking liquid for loosening if too thick. Serve hot alongside fish and chips.

Notes
