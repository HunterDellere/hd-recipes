Chick-fil-A sauce (homemade copycat)

The signature honey-mustard-barbecue blend, built from a quality mayonnaise base with smoked paprika, mustard, honey, and a touch of lemon. Tastes cleaner than the franchise version because the mayo is fresh and the smoke comes from actual smoked paprika rather than liquid smoke.

Mise en place by phase 1 phase ▾

Sauce

  1. 120 g mayonnaise (full-fat, real-egg, ideally homemade)
  2. 30 g honey
  3. 15 g Dijon mustard
  4. 8 g yellow mustard (American)
  5. 15 g barbecue sauce (or homemade)
  6. 1 g smoked paprika
  7. 5 ml fresh lemon juice
  8. 0.5 g fine sea salt — (pinch; adjust based on mayo saltiness)

Execution

  1. 1
    In a small bowl combine the 120 g / ½ cup mayonnaise, 30 g / 2 tbsp honey, 15 g / 1 tbsp Dijon mustard, 8 g / ½ tbsp yellow mustard, 15 g / 1 tbsp barbecue sauce, 1 g / ¼ tsp smoked paprika, 5 ml / 1 tsp lemon juice, and a 0.5 g pinch salt.
  2. 2
    Whisk vigorously for 30 seconds until completely smooth and uniformly pale gold. The smoked paprika should fully dissolve, not show as orange specks; if specks remain, push them with the back of the spoon to disperse.
  3. 3
    Taste. Adjust by quarter-teaspoon increments: more honey if it reads sharp, more mustard if it reads flat, more lemon if it reads heavy. The franchise version is calibrated as roughly equal sweet-tangy with smoke and salt as supporting notes; aim there.
  4. 4
    Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes before serving, the flavors integrate dramatically with even a short rest, and the texture sets up slightly thicker. Holds 1 week refrigerated in a sealed jar.

Modifications

Pivots that change the dish's character — protein swaps, regional variants, dietary recastings.

  • Heat flavor→spicy Chick-fil-A sauce

    Add ½ tsp sriracha and ¼ tsp cayenne. The franchise spicy version is less common; this is a built-from-scratch upgrade.

  • Regional flavor→extra-tangy version (Texas-leaning)

    Double the yellow mustard to 16 g / 1 tbsp and add 1 tsp apple cider vinegar. Brighter, more mustard-forward, closer to a Carolina mustard BBQ.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • mayonnaise → Greek yogurt for a lighter version Replaces ⅔ of the mayo (80 g yogurt + 40 g mayo). Loses some of the rich body but cuts fat substantially. The franchise version is full-mayo; this is a deliberate compromise.
  • barbecue sauce → molasses + Worcestershire (1 tbsp + ½ tsp) If no BBQ sauce on hand, this DIY hits the same sweet-tangy-smoky note. Adds an extra layer of complexity actually.
  • honey → maple syrup Slightly different sweet profile but works. Use 1:1.

Make it from scratch

Store-bought is fine, but each of these has a homemade version that earns the time.

  • → mayonnaise (full-fat, real-egg, ideally homemade) make it yourselfstub Fresh real-egg mayo is dramatically better than jarred, cleaner egg flavor, no preservatives, and the sauce reads less commercial when built on it.

Notes

The franchise Chick-fil-A sauce is honey-mustard-BBQ at roughly equal parts on a mayo base. The exact ratio is a guarded company secret but every clone recipe published online lands within a few grams of these numbers. The interesting variable is mayonnaise quality, Hellmann's (or your own homemade mayo) makes a noticeably better sauce than the cheap restaurant mayo most chain franchises use.

The smoked paprika is the wildcard. Many copycat recipes call for liquid smoke; this version uses real smoked paprika because the flavor compound is more nuanced and the recipe stays in a cleaner ingredient set. If you must use liquid smoke, use 1 drop and skip the paprika, liquid smoke is potent and a few drops too many ruin the sauce.

The 30-minute refrigerated rest matters. The honey takes time to integrate with the mustard's acid; freshly mixed sauce tastes harshly mustard-forward and reads as imbalanced. After 30 minutes the components soften into each other and the sauce reads as a unified emulsion rather than parts. After 24 hours it tastes even better, make it the day before if possible.
