Smashed cucumber and wood ear salad

Crushed Persian cucumbers and rehydrated wood ear in a Chinkiang vinegar dressing — sharp, snappy, the right counter to anything heavy.

Mise en place by phase 2 phases ▾

Vegetables

  1. 600 g Persian cucumbers — (6 small)
  2. 300 g wood ear mushrooms, rehydrated and trimmed
  3. 6 g fine sea salt

Dressing

  1. 60 g Chinkiang black vinegar
  2. 10 g sugar
  3. 15 g light soy sauce
  4. 3 each garlic, grated — (3 cloves)
  5. 15 g toasted sesame oil
  6. 5 g chili oil optional

Execution

  1. 1
    Lay the 600 g / 6 small Persian cucumbers on a board. Smash them with the side of a heavy knife or a rolling pin until they split and flatten. Cut into rough 1-inch chunks. The jagged edges grab the dressing in a way that smooth slices can't.
  2. 2
    Toss the cucumbers and 300 g rehydrated wood ear with 6 g / 1 tsp fine sea salt in a colander. Let sit 15 minutes. The salt pulls cellular water out, so the salad won't weep into the dressing later.
  3. 3
    In a separate bowl, whisk 60 g / 4 tbsp Chinkiang vinegar, 10 g / 2 tsp sugar, 15 g / 1 tbsp light soy sauce, and 3 grated garlic cloves until the sugar dissolves.
  4. 4
    Slowly whisk in 15 g / 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil and the optional 5 g / 1 tsp chili oil if using. The oil should suspend in a thin film over the vinegar base.
  5. 5
    Pat the cucumbers and 300 g wood ear mushrooms dry with a paper towel. Toss them into the dressing, making sure the folds in the wood ear get coated; they hold flavor better than anything else in the bowl.
  6. 6
    Taste. If too sharp, another 2 g pinch of sugar. If flat, more salt or a drop of fish sauce. Serve cold.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • plain version → + 2 tbsp toasted sliced almonds or 1 tsp sesame seeds Adds a secondary crunch. Good with dandan as a counter to the peanuts.
  • light soy sauce → coconut aminos + reduce the salt step to 5 minutes Lower-sodium version.

Make it from scratch

Store-bought is fine, but each of these has a homemade version that earns the time.

  • → chili oil make it yourself Just a teaspoon, but the homemade version's aromatic complexity (Sichuan peppercorn, cassia, star anise) shows up clearly against the cucumber-clean base. A bottled chili oil here reads as just heat.

Notes

Salting before dressing is the only way this stays crisp. If you skip it, the salt in the dressing pulls water from the cucumbers after they''re mixed, and the salad becomes a watery puddle within 10 minutes.

Wood ear has almost no flavor of its own — what it brings is texture: gelatinous, springy, faintly crunchy. It''s a vehicle for the dressing.

Eat within an hour of dressing. The longer it sits, the softer the cucumbers get.
