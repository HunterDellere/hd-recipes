Ginger-garlic paste (homemade adrak-lehsun)

Fresh ginger and garlic blended in roughly equal parts with a small amount of oil and salt to a smooth paste. The structural aromatic base of nearly every South Asian curry, marinade, and stir-fry. Five minutes of work, lasts a week refrigerated. Stub.

Mise en Place

Servings
Units
Shopping list 5 items ▾
  1. 100 g fresh ginger, peeled and rough-chopped — (about 4 inches knob)
  2. 100 g garlic cloves, peeled — (about 25 to 30 large cloves)
  3. 30 ml neutral oil
  4. 4 g kosher salt
  5. 30 ml water (as needed for blending)

Execution

  1. 1
    Stub: combine all ingredients in a small food processor or blender. Pulse-blend, scraping down the sides every 10 seconds, until the paste is uniformly smooth (texture target: a smooth pourable paste, like thick mustard). Add 1 to 2 tbsp water if the blade struggles.
  2. 2
    Stub: transfer to a clean glass jar with a tight lid. Smooth the surface and pour over a thin layer of additional oil (about 1 tsp) to seal the paste against air; this prevents oxidation and extends shelf life. Refrigerate.
