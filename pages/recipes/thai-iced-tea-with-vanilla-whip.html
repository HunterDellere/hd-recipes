Thai iced tea with salted vanilla whip

Spiced black tea sweetened with brown sugar and condensed milk, finished with a soft-peak salted vanilla cream that floats and slowly cascades.

Mise en place by phase 4 phases ▾

Spiced tea base

  1. 480 ml filtered water
  2. 6 g loose-leaf black tea (Assam or Ceylon)
  3. 3 each star anise
  4. 3 each green cardamom pods, crushed
  5. 1 each cinnamon stick
  6. 2 each whole cloves
  7. 1 each orange peel — (1 strip)
  8. 1 g kosher salt — (pinch)

Sweetener and aromatics

  1. 40 g sweetened condensed milk
  2. 13 g dark brown sugar (or turbinado)
  3. 2.5 g vanilla extract
  4. 1 g almond extract
  5. 1 g orange blossom water

Salted vanilla whip

  1. 120 ml heavy cream, very cold
  2. 4 g powdered sugar
  3. 0.3 g kosher salt — (tiny pinch)
  4. 2.5 g vanilla bean paste

Service

  1. to taste crushed or pebble ice

Execution

  1. 1
    Toast 3 star anise pods, 3 crushed cardamom pods, 1 cinnamon stick, and 2 whole cloves in a dry saucepan over medium for 1–2 minutes, shaking constantly, until they smell sharp and warm.
  2. 2
    Add 480 ml / 2 cups filtered water, 1 strip orange peel, and a 1 g pinch kosher salt. Bring to a hard boil, cover, and cook 5 minutes.
  3. 3
    Pull off the heat and let it stop bubbling, about 30 seconds. Stir in 6 g / 2 tbsp loose-leaf black tea. Cover and steep exactly 4 minutes.
  4. 4
    Strain through a fine sieve into a heatproof pitcher. Don't squeeze the leaves; that releases tannins.
  5. 5
    Whisk in 13 g / 1 tbsp dark brown sugar and 40 g / 2 tbsp sweetened condensed milk while still hot. Then stir in 2.5 g / ½ tsp vanilla extract, 1 g / ¼ tsp almond extract, and 1 g / ¼ tsp orange blossom water (added off-heat to preserve the volatiles).
  6. 6
    Chill the base in the fridge until cold (or shock in the freezer for 20 minutes). A cold base means the ice doesn't melt instantly.
  7. 7
    In a small cold bowl, whisk 120 ml / ½ cup heavy cream with 4 g / 1 tsp powdered sugar, a 0.3 g pinch kosher salt, and 2.5 g / ½ tsp vanilla bean paste 45–60 seconds to soft peaks. The cream should hold its shape when you lift the whisk but still slump like wet paint, not stiff like shaving cream.
  8. 8
    Fill glasses with ice. Pour the cold tea ¾ full. Spoon the salted whip over the top — it floats, then slowly cascades down through the tea.

Substitutions

1:1 ingredient swaps where the dish stays the same dish.

  • salted vanilla whip → + pinch of instant coffee powder "Dirty" mocha-style finish.
  • heavy cream → thick top-of-the-can coconut cream Dairy-free version. Whip with the same powdered sugar.
  • plain tea base → + 1 oz spiced rum or bourbon Adult version. Add at the cooled-base stage.

Notes

Brown sugar over white because the molasses shares phenolic flavor compounds with vanilla and toasted spice, which makes the tea taste deeper and less cloying than the same drink built on white sugar.

The tiny pinch of salt in the whip is doing real work. It cuts the cream''s fat and contrasts the sweet tea, hitting the same circuitry as salted caramel.

For an authentic frothy texture under the whip, "pull" the cooled base by pouring it back and forth between two pitchers a few times before serving over ice.
