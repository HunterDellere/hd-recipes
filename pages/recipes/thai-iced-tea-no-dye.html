Thai iced tea (no dye)

Small-batch Thai iced tea built on whole spices and loose-leaf black tea — no orange food coloring, low astringency, evaporated milk float.

2 servings35 min total15 min activeeasyThaidrink
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Tea base

  1. 480 ml water — (2 cups)
  2. 6 g loose-leaf black tea (Assam, Ceylon, or English Breakfast) — (2 tbsp)
  3. 2 each star anise
  4. 2 each green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  5. 1 each cinnamon stick — (small, or ½ large)
  6. 2 each whole cloves

Sweetener and finish

  1. 25 g granulated sugar — (2 tbsp; adjust)
  2. 40 g sweetened condensed milk — (2 tbsp)
  3. 2.5 g vanilla extract — (½ tsp)
  4. 1 g almond extractoptional — (¼ tsp; mimics the "mix" flavor)

Service

  1. to taste ice cubes
  2. 30 g evaporated milk (or half and half) — (2–4 tbsp)

Execution

  1. 1
    Combine water, star anise, cardamom, cinnamon stick, and cloves in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil, then drop heat slightly and simmer hard for 5 minutes. Spices need active heat to release their oils.6 min
  2. 2
    Pull the pot fully off the heat. Stir in the loose-leaf tea, cover, and steep exactly 5 minutes. Don't go longer — that's where the dry-mouth tannins come in.5 min
  3. 3
    Strain through a fine sieve into a heatproof jar. Discard the spent leaves and spices.1 min
  4. 4
    While still steaming hot, whisk in sugar and condensed milk until fully dissolved. Stir in vanilla and almond extracts.2 min
  5. 5
    Cool to room temperature (about 20 minutes), or chill rapidly in the fridge or freezer.20 min
  6. 6
    Fill two tall glasses to the brim with ice. Pour the cold tea over, leaving an inch of space at the top.1 min
  7. 7
    Float 1–2 tbsp evaporated milk over the top of each glass. It will swirl down through the tea into a gradient. Stir and sip.1 min

Substitutions

  • condensed milk → sweetened condensed coconut milk Dairy-free version. Use full-fat coconut milk for the float, too.
  • natural amber color → pinch of turmeric or a drop of food coloring If you miss the neon orange of the restaurant version. Real Thai tea is amber, not orange.
  • standard strength → 3 tbsp tea (no longer steep time) For drinks you'll sip slowly with a lot of melting ice.

Notes

The split between spice decoction and tea steep is the whole game. Spices are tough wood fibers — they need active heat to release oils. Tea leaves are delicate — boil them and you pull bitter tannins. Two phases, one drink, no astringency.

The condensed milk does double duty. It sweetens and provides casein proteins that bind whatever residual tannins made it through, smoothing the finish. The evaporated milk float adds visual drama plus a fat coating on the tongue that mitigates any remaining bitterness.

Star anise is the non-negotiable note. Without it you have sweet milk tea — pleasant, but not Thai iced tea.
