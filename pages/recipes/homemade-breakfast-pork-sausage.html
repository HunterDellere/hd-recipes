Homemade breakfast pork sausage

Ground pork mixed with sage, brown sugar, black pepper, cayenne, thyme, and nutmeg, rested overnight so the seasonings bloom. The structural Southern breakfast sausage at home, without preservatives and with full control over the spice profile. Stub.

6servingsabout 450 g sausage; enough for one batch of sausage gravy or 8 small patties
10 minactive· overnight

Mise en Place

Servings
Units
Shopping list 8 items ▾
  1. 450 g ground pork (80/20)
  2. 6 g kosher salt
  3. 4 g light brown sugar
  4. 4 g black pepper, freshly ground
  5. 2 g dried sage (rubbed)
  6. 1 g dried thyme
  7. 1 g cayenne pepper
  8. 1 g freshly grated nutmeg

Execution

  1. 1
    Stub: combine all ingredients in a bowl. Mix thoroughly with gloved hands (overmixing develops myosin and makes the sausage rubbery; mix just until evenly distributed).
  2. 2
    Stub: cover tightly with parchment, refrigerate at least 12 hours (the rest blooms the seasonings into the meat). Keeps 3 days refrigerated, freezes 3 months.
  3. 3
    Stub: to use, cook loose in a skillet for sausage gravy (per the biscuits-and-gravy recipe), or form into 50 g patties and pan-fry 3 minutes per side.

Notes
