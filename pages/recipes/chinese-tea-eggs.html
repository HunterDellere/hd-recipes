Chinese tea eggs

Soft-boiled eggs cracked all over and steeped in spiced soy and black tea. The cracks become a marble pattern in the white.

12 servings25 min active+ 1 d resteasyChinesesnack

Eggs

  1. 12 each large eggs — (~600g)

Brine

  1. 960 ml water
  2. 120 g light soy sauce
  3. 30 g dark soy sauce
  4. 15 g rock sugar or granulated sugar

Aromatics

  1. 3 each star anise — (~6g)
  2. 2 each cinnamon sticks — (~10g)
  3. 2 g Sichuan peppercorns

Execution

  1. 1
    Lay the eggs in a single layer in a Dutch oven and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil, then drop to a low simmer for 7 minutes — set yolks but not chalky.9 min
  2. 2
    Transfer to an ice bath for 5 minutes. The egg contracts away from the shell, which is what makes the next step easy.5 min
  3. 3
    Tap each shell all over with the back of a spoon until you see a fine network of cracks. Do not peel. The cracks are the channels for the brine.5 min
  4. 4
    In the same Dutch oven, combine water, both soy sauces, sugar, star anise, cinnamon sticks, Sichuan peppercorns, and tea leaves. Bring to a simmer.5 min
  5. 5
    Submerge the cracked eggs. Bring back to a bare simmer, then cut the heat. Cover and let steep at least 4 hours at room temperature, or (better) refrigerate and steep 24 hours. Longer steep, deeper marbling and flavor.1 d 0 h
  6. 6
    Peel one to check the marble pattern and the seasoning. If the flavor is faint, return the rest to the brine for another 4 hours.2 min

Substitutions

  • Pu-erh or Ceylon black tea → Lapsang Souchong Smoke-dried leaves give the eggs a campfire note that pairs well with Sichuan dishes.
  • soy sauces → coconut aminos (¾ cup) + 1 tsp extra salt Soy-free version. Color comes out lighter; the savory-sweet balance survives.

Notes

The marbling is capillary action plus osmosis. The cracked shell wicks the dark brine into the membrane, and over hours the salt and color migrate inward through the semi-permeable layer. Time, not heat, does the work.

The tannins in black tea act as a mordant — they help the soy melanoidins fix to the egg-white proteins. Skip the tea and the marbling washes off.

Tea eggs keep submerged in their brine for a week refrigerated. The flavor only deepens.
