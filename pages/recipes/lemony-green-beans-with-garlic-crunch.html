Recipe

Lemony green beans with garlic crunch

Blanched green beans tossed with butter and lemon, finished with a garlicky panko crumb that stays crisp on top.

6 servings25 min total20 min activeeasyAmericanside
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Beans

  1. 680 g fresh green beans, ends trimmed — (1.5 lb)
  2. 15 g kosher salt, for the water — (1 tbsp)
  3. 15 g unsalted butter, for the sauté — (1 tbsp)

Garlic crumb

  1. 45 g unsalted butter — (3 tbsp)
  2. 14 g olive oil — (1 tbsp; keeps the butter from burning)
  3. 40 g panko breadcrumbs — (⅔ cup)
  4. 3 each garlic, finely minced — (3 cloves)
  5. 2 g fresh lemon zest — (1 tsp; ~half a lemon)
  6. 0.5 g black pepper — (¼ tsp)
  7. 1 g salt — (pinch)

Finish

  1. 5 g fresh lemon juice — (1 tsp)
  2. 12 g parmesan, gratedoptional — (2 tbsp)

Execution

  1. 1
    Bring a large pot of water to a hard boil with the salt — it should taste like seawater. Set a bowl of ice water nearby.6 min
  2. 2
    Drop the beans in the boiling water. Boil 3–5 minutes, until tender with a slight snap. Pull one and bite.4 min
  3. 3
    Transfer beans straight into the ice bath. Once fully cold (about 2 minutes), drain and pat dry on a towel.3 min
  4. 4
    In a large skillet over medium heat, melt 3 tbsp butter with the olive oil. Add the panko and stir constantly for 2–3 minutes until light gold.3 min
  5. 5
    Add the minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Stir another 1–2 minutes until both garlic and crumbs are deep golden and fragrant. Watch the garlic — it goes from raw to burnt in seconds.2 min
  6. 6
    Pull off heat. Stir in the lemon zest off the heat (it would bitter on direct contact). Transfer crumbs to a small bowl.1 min
  7. 7
    Wipe the skillet, set back on medium-high, melt 1 tbsp butter. Add the dried beans and toss 2–3 minutes until hot and starting to blister in spots. Add lemon juice; toss once more.3 min
  8. 8
    Cut the heat. Add half the crumb (and parmesan if using) to the pan; toss to coat. Plate, then top with the remaining crumb so the top layer stays crisp. Serve immediately.2 min

Substitutions

  • panko → crushed gluten-free crackers or coarsely chopped toasted almonds Gluten-free option. Almonds add nuttier depth.
  • butter → good olive oil or plant-based butter Dairy-free option.
  • plain crumb → + 2 strips finely chopped cooked bacon Smoky-salty Midwestern variation.

Notes

Blanching in heavily salted water cooks the bean and seasons it from the inside. The shock in ice water locks in the bright green — chlorophyll stays intact when cooked fast and stopped fast. Skip the shock and the beans turn olive drab as residual heat keeps cooking them.

Why panko: the flakes are larger and airier than standard breadcrumbs, so they absorb less fat and stay crisp longer.

Lemon zest carries the limonene — the smell of lemon — without the sour bite. Juice is the acid; zest is the perfume. Most of the lemon character belongs to the zest, in the topping; just a teaspoon of juice goes into the pan to cut the butter.
