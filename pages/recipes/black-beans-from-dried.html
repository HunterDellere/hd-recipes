Black beans from dried

Dried black beans soaked overnight then simmered with onion, bay, and a strip of kombu into a creamy bean and a richly seasoned bean liquor. The structural pot of beans for Mexican, Cuban, and African-American cooking. Stub.

Mise en Place

Servings
Units
Shopping list 8 items ▾
  1. 500 g dried black beans
  2. 2000 ml cold water for soaking
  3. 2500 ml cold water for cooking
  4. 8 g kosher salt
  5. 200 g white onion, halved root-on — (1 medium onion; root-on holds the halves together through the simmer)
  6. 2 each bay leaves
  7. 5 g kombu, dried — (1 × 5 cm strip; the alginates soften the bean skins faster and add background glutamate; not traditionally Mexican but functionally excellent)
  8. 12 g garlic clove, smashed — (3 cloves; whole-cloves smashed not minced, for background flavor without raw allium dominance)

Execution

  1. 1
    Stub: rinse the 500 g / 2½ cups dried beans in cold water, picking out any stones or shriveled beans. Cover with the 2 L / 8 cups cold soak water in a large bowl; stand 8 to 12 hours at room temperature. The beans will roughly double in volume and the soak water will turn dark from leached anthocyanins; this is normal.
  2. 2
    Stub: drain the soak water (discard, the oligosaccharides that cause digestive trouble are concentrated in this water). Transfer the soaked beans to a heavy 5 L pot. Add the 2.5 L / 10 cups fresh cold cooking water, the 8 g / 2 tsp kosher salt, the 200 g / 1 halved white onion, the 2 bay leaves, the 5 g / 5 cm kombu strip, and the 12 g / 3 smashed garlic cloves.
  3. 3
    Stub: bring the pot to a vigorous boil for 5 minutes (the rapid boil sets the bean structure and helps even cooking). Drop to a bare simmer (small bubbles breaking the surface, not a rolling boil); partially cover with the lid offset to allow steam to escape. Simmer 60 to 75 minutes, checking water level every 20 minutes and topping up with hot water as needed (the water should always cover the beans by at least 2 cm). The beans are done when one bean smashed between thumb and forefinger crushes to a creamy paste with no firm interior; the broth should have thickened slightly and turned a deep inky black.
  4. 4
    Stub: pull from heat. Remove and discard the onion, bay leaves, and kombu (or compost). The garlic cloves can be left in the pot or removed. Cool the beans in their cooking liquid; the beans absorb additional broth as they cool and the cooked-bean texture is meaningfully better than straight-from-the-pot. Refrigerate within 2 hours of cooking; the beans plus liquor keep 5 days refrigerated and freeze well for 6 months in 250 g portions.

Notes

This is a stub. Flesh out: the soak chemistry (oligosaccharide leaching), the no-soak alternative method (direct cold-start, 90 to 120 minutes total), the salt-tough-beans myth (false at typical home doses), the kombu mechanism (alginate softens skins via calcium chelation), the Cuban variation (sherry, sofrito, sugar at the end), the Brazilian feijoada variation (multiple cuts of pork), the freezing strategy (cube the beans for sauce thinning, deli containers for soup base), the bean-liquor uses (rice cooking liquid, soup base, sauce thinner, dipping bread).
