Recipe

House blend chai

A small-batch masala chai built on a freshly ground whole-spice blend. Two cups, fifteen minutes, no mix.

2 servings13 min total10 min activeeasyIndiandrink
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Masala blend

  1. 1 each black cardamom pod
  2. 3 each green cardamom pods
  3. 4 each whole cloves
  4. 1 each cinnamon stick — ½-inch piece
  5. 2 each mace petals
  6. 8 each black peppercorns
  7. 0.125 tsp fresh-ground nutmeg

Brew

  1. 360 ml water — (1½ cups)
  2. 1 each fresh ginger — 1-inch knob
  3. 5 g Assam tea, loose leaf — (2 tsp)
  4. 240 ml whole milk — (1 cup)
  5. 5 g sugar — (1 tsp, to taste)

Execution

  1. 1
    Toast the whole spices in a dry small saucepan over low heat for about a minute, until they smell sharp and warm.2 min
  2. 2
    Crush the toasted spices in a mortar — or just mash with the back of a spoon — and combine with the ground nutmeg.1 min
  3. 3
    Add water, the masala blend, and the smashed ginger knob. Bring to a boil over medium-high and let it bubble hard for 3–4 minutes.4 min
  4. 4
    Add the tea leaves and boil 2–3 minutes. The brew should darken to a deep mahogany.3 min
  5. 5
    Fish out the ginger, then pour in the milk. Bring back up to a boil; just before it climbs the pan, pull off the heat. Return to heat and let it climb again. Repeat 2–3 times — this is the pulling that gives chai its body.3 min
  6. 6
    Pull off, stir in sugar to taste, strain through a fine sieve into cups. Serve hot — with cake rusk or roti if you have it.1 min

Notes

The pull-and-return on the milk does real work. Each near-boil concentrates the brew slightly and develops the cooked-milk note that separates a proper chai from black tea with milk in it.

Black cardamom is non-optional for the smoky note. If you can't find it, double the green and add a single broken bay leaf to compensate.
