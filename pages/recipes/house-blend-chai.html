House blend chai

A small-batch masala chai built on a freshly ground whole-spice blend. Two cups, fifteen minutes, no mix.

Mise en place by phase 2 phases ▾

Masala blend

  1. 1 each black cardamom pod
  2. 3 each green cardamom pods
  3. 4 each whole cloves
  4. 1 each cinnamon stick — ½-inch piece
  5. 2 each mace petals
  6. 8 each black peppercorns
  7. 0.125 tsp fresh-ground nutmeg

Brew

  1. 360 ml water
  2. 1 each fresh ginger — 1-inch knob
  3. 5 g Assam tea, loose leaf
  4. 240 ml whole milk
  5. 5 g sugar

Execution

  1. 1
    Toast the whole spices in a dry small saucepan over low heat for about a minute, until they smell sharp and warm.
  2. 2
    Crush the toasted spices in a mortar (or just mash with the back of a spoon) and combine with the ground nutmeg.
  3. 3
    Add 360 ml / 1½ cups water, the masala blend, and the smashed 1-inch ginger knob. Bring to a boil over medium-high and let it bubble hard for 3–4 minutes.
  4. 4
    Add 5 g / 2 tsp Assam tea leaves and boil 2–3 minutes. The brew should darken to a deep mahogany.
  5. 5
    Fish out the ginger knob, then pour in 240 ml / 1 cup whole milk. Bring back up to a boil; just before it climbs the pan, pull off the heat. Return to heat and let it climb again. Repeat 2–3 times — this is the pulling that gives chai its body.
  6. 6
    Pull off, stir in 5 g / 1 tsp sugar (to taste), strain through a fine sieve into cups. Serve hot — with cake rusk or roti if you have it.

Notes

The pull-and-return on the milk does real work. Each near-boil concentrates the brew slightly and develops the cooked-milk note that separates a proper chai from black tea with milk in it.

Black cardamom is non-optional for the smoky note. If you can't find it, double the green and add a single broken bay leaf to compensate.
