Brown butter sugar cookies

Sugar cookies built on melted brown butter — chewy and dense in the center, with a crackled sugar shell on top.

12 servings22 min total12 min activeeasydessert
vegetarian

Mise en Place

Servings

Cookie dough

  1. 115 g unsalted butter — (1 stick / 8 tbsp)
  2. 150 g granulated sugar — (¾ cup)
  3. 55 g light brown sugar — (¼ cup, packed)
  4. 1 each large egg
  5. 5 g vanilla bean paste — (1 tsp; or 2 tsp extract)
  6. 165 g all-purpose flour — (1⅓ cups)
  7. 3 g baking soda — (½ tsp)
  8. 3 g fine sea salt — (½ tsp)

Coating

  1. 65 g granulated sugar, for rolling — (⅓ cup)

Execution

  1. 1
    Brown the butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat. It will foam, sizzle, then go quiet as the milk solids drop and turn amber. Pull when you smell toasted hazelnut, about 4 minutes. Pour into a large heatproof bowl and let cool 5 minutes. Brown butter (beurre noisette)5 min
  2. 2
    Whisk the granulated and brown sugars into the warm butter for a full minute. The mixture looks gritty; that's correct.2 min
  3. 3
    Whisk in the egg and vanilla. Beat hard for 30–60 seconds until glossy and ribbon-thick. This is what creates the crackle on top.1 min
  4. 4
    Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt with a spatula. Mix until the streaks just disappear.1 min
  5. 5
    Heat oven to 350°F (175°C). Scoop dough into 1.5-tbsp balls, roll generously in the reserved sugar, and space 2 inches apart on a parchment-lined sheet.5 min
  6. 6
    Bake 10–12 minutes. At the 8-minute mark, lift the pan an inch and drop it on the rack: this collapses the centers and gives you the rippled top.12 min
  7. 7
    Pull when edges are set and golden but centers look underbaked. Cool on the pan 10 minutes; the residual heat finishes the chew.10 min

Substitutions

  • all-purpose flour → self-rising flour Omit the baking soda and salt.
  • plain dough → + 1 tsp cardamom and ½ tsp cinnamon Chai-spiced variation.
  • sugar coating → sprinkles Birthday-cake version.

Notes

Why melted butter, not creamed: creaming whips air into the dough and gives you a lifted, cakey cookie. Melting collapses that — the protein network packs denser, and you get the fudgy chew you actually want from a sugar cookie.

Why baking soda (not powder): brown sugar is acidic enough on its own to activate soda, and soda is alkaline enough to push more browning. More color, more flavor, slightly thinner spread.

The white/brown sugar split matters. White sugar promotes crisp edges; the molasses in brown sugar is hygroscopic and keeps the centers soft for days.
