Ingredient

Cinnamon stick

Dried inner bark of Cinnamomum trees. Two main types: hot, sharp Cassia (the supermarket default) and floral, gentler Ceylon ("true" cinnamon).

About

Two species are sold as "cinnamon" and they aren't interchangeable. Cassia (Cinnamomum cassia) is what almost all US grocery cinnamon is — thick, hard, dark bark with high cinnamaldehyde content and a sharp, woody heat. Ceylon (Cinnamomum verum) is paler, brittle, with thin layers, and a softer, more complex citrus-floral character.

Cassia stands up to long simmers (chai, mulled wine, braises). Ceylon shines where you can taste the cinnamon directly (rice pudding, custards, fine pastry).

Sticks release flavor more slowly than ground but don''t go stale as fast. For a chai or tea, snap a stick in half before adding to expose more surface area.

Storage

Airtight jar, dark pantry. Sticks: 2+ years. Ground: 6 months at most before the volatile oils fade.

Substitutions

  • cinnamon stick → 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon Per 3-inch stick. Add late and strain — ground will sediment.
