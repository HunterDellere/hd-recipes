Ingredient

Mung bean (split, hulled)

Small yellow split mung beans (moong dal). Cook fast into a smooth, sweet, slightly buttery paste, the workhorse legume of South and Southeast Asian sweet preparations.

About

Mung beans (Vigna radiata) sold for cooking come in three forms: whole green (skins on), split green (skin on but cracked), and split yellow (skin off, cotyledons separated). The split yellow is the form used in Vietnamese chè, Filipino hopia, Chinese mooncake fillings, and Indian moong dal, it cooks in 20 to 30 minutes without soaking and breaks down into a smooth paste that takes sweetener cleanly.

The flavor is delicate: lightly nutty, slightly green-vegetal, with a clean finish that does not interfere with coconut, palm sugar, or pandan. This neutrality is why mung bean became the standard filling-and-base legume across so many cuisines; it carries other flavors better than red bean (sweeter, beanier), black bean (more assertive, earthy), or chickpea (savory, starchy).

For paste applications: cook split yellow mung beans in water at a 1:3 ratio until soft (~25 min), drain, mash or process while warm. Add sugar (or palm sugar) after mashing, adding it during cooking slows softening because sugar competes with starch for water. For paste filling stability, dry the cooked beans in a pan over low heat before mashing; excess moisture makes the filling slump and the wrapping pastry sog.

Soaking is optional for split mung beans (whole mung benefit from 4 hours of soak). Pre-soaking shortens cook time by 5 minutes but doesn't meaningfully improve texture.

Storage

Airtight container, cool dark pantry. One year for split beans; older beans cook unevenly and may never fully soften, use within season for paste applications.

Substitutions

What to reach for when this ingredient is unavailable. Each row names the use case so the swap is judged in context.

  • split yellow mung bean (chè filling, mooncake) → no good substitute Red bean, adzuki, and white kidney pastes all carry distinct flavors that change the dish identity. If you need the mild backdrop, find the mung bean.
  • whole green mung bean (chè sweet soup) → split yellow mung bean Yellow split lacks the skin texture. Reduce cook time to 25 min and expect a smoother, more uniform body in the soup.
