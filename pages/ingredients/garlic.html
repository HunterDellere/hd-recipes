Ingredient

Garlic

Allium sativum. Pungency comes from allicin, which only forms when you cut or crush a clove — and which mellows quickly with heat.

About

Garlic''s sharp bite is allicin — and allicin doesn''t exist in an intact clove. It forms when you damage cell walls (cut, crush, grate), which lets the enzyme alliinase react with the precursor alliin. The more you damage the clove, the more allicin you generate. A whole clove poached in milk is sweet and almost nutty; the same clove minced raw is hot enough to bite. Allicin breaks down with heat. Sweat minced garlic in oil for 30 seconds and most of the harshness is gone. Cook it past golden and you''re into bitter territory. Pre-minced jarred garlic is a flat, slightly metallic shadow of the real thing. The chemistry has already happened and stalled; you''re tasting day-old allicin breakdown products. Buy whole heads, mince fresh.

Storage

Cool dark place with airflow — never the fridge (turns rubbery). Whole heads keep 1–2 months. Once a head is broken open, individual cloves last 1–2 weeks.
