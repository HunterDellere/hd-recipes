Ingredient

Assam tea

Robust, malty black tea from northeast India. The default base for masala chai and what gives English Breakfast its backbone.

About

Assam is grown in the Brahmaputra valley and produced from Camellia sinensis var. assamica — a different varietal from the Chinese sinensis used in most other black teas. The leaves are larger and the resulting brew is darker, more tannic, and distinctly malty. This robustness is the point in chai: Assam stands up to milk, sugar, and heavy spicing without disappearing. Lighter Darjeelings or Chinese black teas get drowned. CTC (crush-tear-curl) Assam — the small pellet form — extracts faster and is what most Indian households actually use. For chai, 5–7g per cup. Steep no longer than 4 minutes off the boil; longer pulls bitter tannins.

Storage

Airtight tin, dark cupboard. 6–12 months. Loses character past a year.

Substitutions

  • Assam tea → Ceylon or English Breakfast Both work in chai. Ceylon is brighter, English Breakfast usually contains Assam anyway.
