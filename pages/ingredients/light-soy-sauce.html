Ingredient

Light soy sauce (生抽)

The everyday Chinese seasoning soy — saltier, thinner, and lighter in color than dark soy. This is what most stir-fries and dipping sauces actually want.

About

Light soy sauce (sheng chou / 生抽) is the first pressing of soy fermentation. It carries most of the salt and most of the glutamates: that's the umami.

Don't confuse it with "low-sodium soy sauce." Light here means light in color and body, not light in salt — light soy is actually the saltiest of the soy sauces. If a Chinese recipe says soy sauce without qualification, this is what it means.

Brands worth seeking out: Pearl River Bridge, Lee Kum Kee Premium, Kimlan. Japanese shoyu (Kikkoman) is a usable substitute but slightly sweeter and more aromatic; works in a pinch but will shift the flavor of strictly Chinese dishes.

Storage

Pantry, capped. Refrigerate after opening for best flavor; oxidation dulls the brightness over a few months.

Substitutions

  • light soy sauce → shoyu (Japanese soy) Slightly sweeter and more delicate. Fine in most Chinese applications.
  • light soy sauce → tamari Gluten-free option. Richer and less salty; use about 10% more.
