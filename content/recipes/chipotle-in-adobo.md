steps:
  - text: 'Stub: in a dry comal or cast-iron skillet over medium heat, toast the 60 g / 15 to 18 chipotle peppers for 30 to 60 seconds per side until they puff slightly and release a pronounced toasted-chipotle aroma. Do not let them blacken; scorched peppers turn the adobo bitter. Pull to a heatproof bowl.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Stub: pour the 500 ml / 2 cups just-boiled water over the toasted peppers. Weight with a small plate to keep them submerged. Let stand 20 minutes; the peppers will rehydrate to soft and pliable.'
    time_min: 22
  - text: 'Stub: drain the rehydrated peppers (reserve 100 ml / ½ cup of the soaking water). In a blender, combine the rehydrated peppers, the reserved 100 ml soaking water, the 200 g tomatoes, the 80 g / ⅓ cup apple cider vinegar, the 100 g / ½ medium quartered onion, the 20 g / 5 garlic cloves, the 8 g / 2 tsp kosher salt, the 12 g / 1 tbsp piloncillo, the 1 g / ½ tsp Mexican oregano, the 1 g / ½ tsp ground cumin, and the 1 bay leaf (snap in half before adding so it blends smoothly). Blend on high for 60 to 90 seconds until completely smooth; the mixture should be a uniform deep red-brown paste.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Stub: heat the 20 g / 1½ tbsp oil in a heavy 2 L saucepan over medium heat. Add the blended adobo paste; it will spit aggressively as the moisture hits the hot oil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent scorching at the bottom. The paste deepens in color and concentrates as moisture evaporates; the final product should be a thick spoonable paste, slightly looser than tomato paste, with the pepper pulp visible.'
    time_min: 12
  - text: 'Stub: pull from heat; cool to room temperature. Transfer to a sealed glass jar; refrigerated, the adobo keeps 1 month. For longer storage, freeze in 30 g / 2 tbsp portions in an ice cube tray, then transfer cubes to a zip-top freezer bag; frozen for 6 months. Use spoonful-for-spoonful as a substitute for canned chipotle in adobo.'
    time_min: 5
