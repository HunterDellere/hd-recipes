steps:
  - text: 'In a small saucepan combine the 80 g / ⅓ cup ketchup, 40 g / 3 tbsp sugar, 30 ml / 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar, 15 g / 1 tbsp neutral oil, and 30 ml / 2 tbsp water. Whisk to a uniform liquid.'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Warm over medium-low heat for 3 to 4 minutes, whisking continuously, just until the sugar fully dissolves and the sauce looks glossy. Do not boil, sustained high heat caramelizes the sugar and the sauce takes on a burnt edge.'

notes: |
  The franchise Polynesian sauce is essentially a French dressing crossed with a sweet-and-sour Chinese restaurant pour sauce. The orange color comes from the ketchup base plus the smoked paprika. Most copycat recipes online add a drop of orange food coloring; this version skips it because the natural color is already close.

  Warm-then-cool method matters because the sugar dose is high. Cold mixing leaves undissolved sugar crystals that taste gritty; sustained boiling caramelizes the sugar to a darker flavor. The brief warm-and-whisk dissolves cleanly without color change.

  The sauce is meant to read sweet. The franchise calibration is sugar-forward; if you find it cloying, drop the sugar to 25 g / 2 tbsp and add an extra teaspoon of vinegar to compensate. Bear in mind that as a dipping sauce for nuggets the salt and protein of the chicken counterbalances; tasted alone from the spoon, it will always read sweeter than it does in service.
