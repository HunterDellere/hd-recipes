steps:
  - text: 'Stub: cold-soak the kombu in the cold water in a 2-quart saucepan for 30 minutes (room temperature) or overnight (refrigerated). The glutamate releases slowly from the kombu into the cold water during this rest.'
    time_min: 30
  - text: 'Stub: place the saucepan over medium heat. Bring slowly to just under boiling (about 80 to 85°C, small bubbles forming at the pot bottom but no rolling boil), 8 to 10 minutes. Just before the water boils, lift out the kombu with chopsticks; continuing to heat releases bitter compounds. Reserve the kombu for niban dashi (the second extraction) or to slice into ribbons as a vegetable.'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Stub: bring the water to 95°C (just below boiling), sprinkle the 20 g katsuobushi over the surface. The flakes will sink within 30 to 60 seconds as they absorb water. Pull off heat immediately. Let stand 3 to 4 minutes for the inosinate to release into the dashi (a longer steep produces a stronger but slightly more bitter dashi; this short steep is the ichiban style).'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Stub: strain through a fine-mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth into a clean pot or large measuring cup.
