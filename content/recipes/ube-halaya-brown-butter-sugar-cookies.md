before_you_start:
  - 'This is a melted-butter dough carrying ube halaya, which is 30 to 40% water. That extra moisture makes the dough spread flatter and faster than the plain brown butter sugar cookie unless it is firmed first. A 45-minute fridge chill after mixing is the difference between a domed, crackled cookie and a thin, greasy one. Build the chill into your timing.'
  - 'Use jarred Good Shepherd or another muted-violet halaya rather than a fluorescent-purple jar. The fluorescent jars carry added blue and red dye that reads garish against the white sugar shell; the muted jam bakes to the jam-rich violet you actually want here.'

steps:
  - text: 'Brown the 115 g / 1 stick unsalted butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat. It foams, sizzles, then quiets as the milk solids drop and turn amber. Pull it when you smell toasted hazelnut, about 4 minutes. Scrape every brown fleck into a large heatproof bowl and let it cool 5 minutes.'
    technique: 'brown-butter'
    time_min: 9
  - text: 'Whisk the 120 g / ⅔ cup granulated sugar and 40 g / 3 tbsp packed light brown sugar into the warm butter for a full minute. The mixture looks gritty and wet sand; that is correct.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Whisk in the 100 g / scant ½ cup ube halaya until the mass turns even and muted lavender, about 30 seconds. Add the 1 large egg, 4 g / ¾ tsp vanilla bean paste, and the optional 3 ml / ½ tsp ube extract, then beat hard for 30 to 60 seconds until glossy and ribbon-thick. That emulsion is what builds the crackled shell later.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Stir in the 175 g / 1⅓ cups + 1 tbsp all-purpose flour, 15 g / 2 tbsp cornstarch, 3 g / ½ tsp baking soda, and 3 g / ½ tsp fine sea salt with a spatula until the streaks just disappear. The dough is softer and tackier than a plain sugar cookie dough because of the jam water.'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Cover the bowl and chill 45 minutes. The dough firms from spreadable to scoopable, and the fat re-sets so the cookies hold a dome instead of flooding flat. Do not skip this; warm halaya dough spreads thin and greasy.'
    time_min: 45
  - text: 'Heat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line two sheets with parchment. Scoop the chilled dough into 1.5-tbsp balls, roll each generously in the 65 g / ⅓ cup reserved granulated sugar, and space them 2 inches apart.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Bake 11 to 13 minutes. At the 9-minute mark, lift the pan an inch and drop it flat on the rack to collapse the centers and set the rippled top; repeat once more at minute 11 if the cookies still look puffed.'
    time_min: 13
  - text: 'Pull them when the edges are set and the centers still look underbaked and read violet through the cracks. Cool on the pan 10 minutes so the residual heat finishes the chew, then transfer to a rack.'
    time_min: 10

notes: |
  Why this works as a conversion. The plain brown butter sugar cookie is a melted-butter dough: the fat is liquefied so the protein network packs dense and you get fudgy chew instead of cakey lift. Ube halaya slots into that structure cleanly because it carries four things at once, color, sweet jam flavor, dairy fat, and cooked-yam starch, in the same way melted chocolate does in a chocolate crinkle. You are swapping one fat-and-starch-bearing flavor mass for another, which is why the dough still behaves like a sugar cookie.

  Why brown butter specifically pairs with halaya. Browning drives off the butter water and Maillard-toasts the milk solids to hazelnut and butterscotch. Halaya's signature depth comes from the Maillard notes in its sweetened condensed milk. The two caramelizations stack rather than clash, and the toasted-dairy register is what keeps the cookie from reading flatly sweet.

  Why cornstarch and the chill, which the plain version skips. Halaya is 30 to 40% water. Drop that into a melted-butter dough with no compensation and you get more gluten development, more spread, and a greasy thin cookie. Cornstarch (pure starch, zero gluten-forming protein) lowers the effective protein percentage for a more tender bite, and the 45-minute chill re-sets the fat so the dough holds a dome. Together they buy back the structure the jam water would otherwise cost you.

  Relationship to the crinkle. The ube halaya crinkle cookie creams softened butter to whip in air for a tall, cracked, cakey crumb. This one melts the butter and browns it for a dense, chewy, flat-cracked crumb with a sugar shell. Same jam, two opposite butter states, two genuinely different cookies. Bake both to taste the lever.
