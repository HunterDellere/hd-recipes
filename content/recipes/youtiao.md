  Flesh out: the chemistry of the double leavening (yeast + baking powder + baking soda is the canonical youtiao trio, and removing any one collapses the center); the role of overnight refrigeration in extensible gluten development (the dough needs to stretch to 3x without snapping during the fry); the paired-strip technique (the chopstick crease creates a fault line where the two strips bond at the surface and balloon apart inside, producing the iconic split-down-the-middle hollow); the oil temperature window (below 190°C the dough soaks oil before it puffs; above 210°C the surface seals before the inside leavens, producing a dense undercooked center); and the Chinese tradition of pairing youtiao with warm doujiang (soy milk) at breakfast or tearing it into hot congee.

  Common congee-side use: torn into bite-sized pieces and dropped into the hot bowl just before eating, so the bottom soaks halfway and the top stays crisp. The textural contrast against the smooth porridge is the entire reason youtiao earns its breakfast slot.
