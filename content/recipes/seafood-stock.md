steps:
  - text: 'For the Mediterranean (roasted-shell) version, heat oven to 200 °C / 400 °F. Toss the 200 g shrimp shells and 300 g crab shells with the 30 g / 2 tbsp olive oil on a heavy roasting pan. Roast 15 to 20 minutes, until the shells turn deep red-orange and smell powerfully seafood-roasted. Skip this step for a delicate fumet (raw version, used for sole sauces, risotto al frutti di mare); roast for cioppino, bouillabaisse, paella, or any tomato-based seafood preparation.'
    time_min: 20
  - text: 'Transfer the roasted shells (or the unroasted shells, for a fumet) to an 8-quart stockpot along with the 500 g of fish bones and head. Add the 200 g onion, 150 g fennel bulb, 100 g leek, and halved 30 g garlic head. Pour the 250 ml / 1 cup dry white wine into the roasting pan (if used), scrape up any fond, and add to the stockpot.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Add the 2000 ml / 8 cups cold water, 2 bay leaves, 4 g / ¾ tsp peppercorns, 10 g parsley stems, and 3 thyme sprigs. Bring to the threshold of a simmer over medium heat (about 90 °C / 195 °F); skim any foam that rises in the first 10 minutes (gray scum, denatured surface proteins from the bones). Reduce to a bare tremor of a simmer and cook 45 minutes uncovered. Stop at 45 minutes; over-simmering past 60 minutes makes the stock taste bitter and aggressively fishy as the bones break down further.'
    time_min: 50
  - text: 'Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a second pot, pressing the solids lightly to extract liquid. Discard the spent shells and bones. For an even cleaner stock, strain again through cheesecloth. The finished stock should be a deep amber-gold (roasted version) or pale gold (fumet version), aromatic, and lightly viscous; cooled and refrigerated, it should set into a soft jelly within a few hours, the visual signature of properly extracted shellfish stock.'
    time_min: 10
