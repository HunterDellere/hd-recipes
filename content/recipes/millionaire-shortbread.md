steps:
  - text: 'Heat oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a 9-inch / 23 cm square pan with parchment slings on both axes, the bars must lift out cleanly.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'For the shortbread: cream 170 g / ¾ cup soft butter with 75 g / ⅓ cup + 1 tbsp granulated sugar and 2 g / ½ tsp fine sea salt for 90 seconds, the goal is to dissolve the salt and incorporate the sugar evenly, NOT to whip air in. A pale tan, not white-fluffy, is the target. Aerated shortbread bakes domed and fragile.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Add 230 g / 1¾ cups + 2 tbsp all-purpose flour all at once. Mix on low until the dough comes together as a damp, sandy mass that just holds when pressed, ~30 seconds. Stop the moment it cohers; gluten development is the enemy of shortbread''s clean snap.'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Press the dough firmly and evenly into the pan. Use the flat bottom of a measuring cup to level it. Dock with a fork at 2 cm intervals, this lets steam escape and prevents the base from doming or cracking.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Bake 22–25 minutes, until the edges are deep gold and the center is set with a pale gold cast. Underbaked shortbread is the most common error: the base must be fully crisp through to support the caramel without going soggy. Let cool to room temperature in the pan.'
    time_min: 25
  - text: 'For the caramel: combine 115 g / ½ cup butter, 100 g / ½ cup packed light brown sugar, 397 g / 1 can sweetened condensed milk, 60 g / 3 tbsp golden syrup, and 2 g / ½ tsp fine sea salt in a heavy-bottomed 3-quart saucepan with a candy thermometer clipped to the side. Heat over medium-low, stirring constantly with a flat-edged wooden spoon and reaching every corner of the pan, sugar that touches the dry pan bottom past 110 °C will scorch and contaminate the entire caramel.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Cook the caramel, stirring constantly, until it reaches 118 °C / 245 °F, the firm-ball stage. This is roughly 12–15 minutes from the first simmer. The color shifts from pale beige through medium tan to a deep amber, peanut-toned color. The 118 °C target is non-negotiable: short of it (110 °C / 230 °F, soft-ball) and the caramel runs at room temperature; over it (125 °C / 257 °F, hard-ball) and the bar is hard-candy stiff and cracks when bitten.'
    time_min: 14
  - text: 'Pull from heat. Stir in 5 g / 1 tsp vanilla bean paste. Pour the caramel onto the cooled shortbread base, working quickly, it sets as it cools. Tap the pan on the counter twice to level. Refrigerate uncovered until fully firm, at least 60 minutes.'
    time_min: 65
  - text: 'For the chocolate top: temper 200 g / 7 oz finely chopped dark chocolate. Melt two-thirds (135 g) over a 50 °C bain-marie until it reads 45–48 °C / 113–118 °F. Pull from heat, stir in the remaining 65 g chopped chocolate, and stir continuously until the mass cools to 31–32 °C / 88–90 °F (for dark chocolate). The chocolate should look slightly thickened and shiny, this is correctly tempered Form V cocoa-butter crystals.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Pour the tempered chocolate over the cold caramel and tilt the pan to spread to every corner. Sprinkle 2 g / ¾ tsp flaky sea salt evenly across the top while the chocolate is still wet, the flakes need to embed for both visual contrast and the controlled salt punch in each bite.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Set at room temperature, NOT in the refrigerator, refrigeration causes the cocoa fat to bloom into Form IV crystals and the chocolate goes dull and streaky. Tempered chocolate sets glossy and snaps cleanly in 30–45 minutes at 18–22 °C. Once set, lift out by the parchment slings.'
    time_min: 40
  - text: 'Trim a thin edge off each side with a long, hot, dry knife (run under hot water, dry, slice; reheat between cuts). Cut into 16 squares (4×4 grid). Each cut should snap the chocolate cleanly, if it cracks unevenly, your temper went out; the bars still taste correct but lose the visual.'
    time_min: 10

notes: |
  The temperature targets are the recipe. Shortbread base baked to deep gold (160 °C × 23 min). Caramel cooked to 118 °C / firm-ball (not 110 °C; not 125 °C). Chocolate tempered to 31–32 °C after a 45 °C melt. Each of these endpoints is what makes its layer behave correctly at room temperature in a stacked bar — a soft-ball caramel oozes, an under-baked base goes soggy under it, and untempered chocolate blooms and snaps wet. The bar is a thermal stack, not a sequence of recipes.

  Why temper chocolate at all: a chocolate top that has not been tempered will set, but it sets in unstable Form IV crystals — soft, dull, streaky, and prone to fingerprint smudging. Tempering forces Form V cocoa-butter crystals: glossy, snap-clean, hard-set, and stable up to 32 °C. This is also why the bars must NOT be refrigerated to set: cold-setting kicks the cocoa butter back to Form IV.

  Why salt the caramel and finish with flaky salt: the caramel''s sweetness is dominated by sucrose and lactose, and at the 60–70% sugar level the bar lives at, salt is a perceptual lever, not a seasoning. The dissolved fine salt inside the caramel cuts the cloying register; the flaky salt on top hits the tongue intermittently and resets the palate between bites. Both are necessary.

  Storage: airtight at room temperature, 5–7 days. Refrigerator works for longer storage but blooms the chocolate; bring to room temperature 20 minutes before serving and accept the visual hit.
