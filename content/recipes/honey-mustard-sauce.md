steps:
  - text: 'In a small bowl whisk together the 60 g / ¼ cup Dijon mustard, 45 g / 2½ tbsp honey, 30 g / 2 tbsp mayonnaise, 10 ml / 2 tsp apple cider vinegar, 0.5 g pinch salt, and 0.5 g pinch black pepper.'

  - text: 'Whisk vigorously for 45 seconds until the sauce is uniform, glossy, and visibly emulsified, no oil streaks, no honey separating to the bottom of the bowl. The texture should be a thick pourable cream.'

  - text: 'Taste. Adjust: more honey if it reads sharp, more vinegar if it reads sleepy, more mustard if it reads candy-sweet. notes: |
  The classic ratio mistake is too much honey. Most fast-food honey-mustard runs at roughly 50 percent sugar by weight, which buries the mustard under syrup. This version runs at about 30 percent, the mustard reads first, the honey rounds, the mayonnaise gives body. If you grew up on commercial honey-mustard, this will taste mustard-forward by comparison; the recalibration is fast.

  Use real Dijon, not yellow mustard or honey-mustard pre-mixed. Yellow mustard is mild, vinegar-forward, and lacks the horseradish-adjacent heat that proper Dijon brings. The yellow color also flattens the visual, a properly built honey mustard should be a warm gold, not a fluorescent yellow.

  Mayonnaise is structural at this small dose. Without it the sauce is too thin and the mustard-honey heat unbalances on the tongue. Adding more shifts the dish toward honey-mustard-mayo, which is closer to a creamy sandwich spread, fine on its own merits but a different sauce.
