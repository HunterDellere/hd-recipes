before_you_start:
  - 'Whisk-and-rest, not whisk-and-serve. The sauce needs at least 30 minutes refrigerated before it reads correctly: the vinegar has to integrate into the mayo emulsion, the relish liquid has to dissolve into the matrix, and the raw garlic and onion powders need time to bloom into the fat. Fresh-mixed Big Mac sauce tastes harshly vinegar-forward and reads as separated parts. After a 30-minute rest the components fuse into the unified pink-orange spread the franchise is calibrated for.'
  - 'For peak result, make it the day before. Day-old Big Mac sauce reads markedly more cohesive than 30-minute sauce; the pickle brine continues to migrate through the emulsion for the first 12 hours.'

steps:
  - text: 'In a small bowl combine the 120 g / ½ cup mayonnaise, 30 g / 2 tbsp sweet pickle relish, 15 g / 1 tbsp yellow mustard, 5 ml / 1 tsp white wine vinegar, 4 g / 1 tsp sugar, 1 g / ½ tsp paprika, 1 g / ½ tsp onion powder, 0.5 g / ¼ tsp garlic powder, and a 0.5 g pinch salt.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Whisk vigorously for 30 seconds until completely smooth and uniformly pale pink-orange. The paprika should fully dissolve into the fat phase rather than show as floating red specks; push any lingering specks with the back of the spoon until the color is even.'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Taste. Adjust by quarter-teaspoon increments: more sugar if it reads sharp, more vinegar if it reads heavy, more mustard if it reads flat. The franchise calibration is sweet-tangy with the relish texture as the dominant mouthfeel; aim there. The garlic and onion powders should register as background warmth, not as discrete flavors.'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes before serving. The flavors integrate dramatically with even a short rest, and the sauce thickens slightly as the relish liquid migrates into the emulsion. Holds 1 week refrigerated in a sealed jar.'
    time_min: 30

notes: |
  The Big Mac sauce ratio published by McDonald''s through various official channels lands within a few grams of these numbers. The core architecture is mayo : relish : mustard at roughly 8 : 2 : 1 by weight, with the vinegar and aromatics as tertiary notes. Most online copycats overdo the garlic powder; the franchise version is restrained there, and a heavier hand tips the sauce toward a generic spread rather than the specific Big Mac profile.

  The relish is the wildcard. Heinz sweet relish is the closest commercial match to what McDonald''s uses; off-brand sweet relishes vary widely in sugar dose and pickle chunk size. If your relish is unusually wet, drain it briefly in a sieve before measuring or the sauce will read thin.

  Paprika selection matters. Use sweet paprika, not smoked. The franchise version has no smoke character at all; smoked paprika is a Chick-fil-A move and reads wrong here. If you only have smoked, use a quarter of the dose and accept the drift.
