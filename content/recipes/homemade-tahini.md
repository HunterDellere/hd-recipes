steps:
  - text: 'Stub: heat the oven to 160°C / 325°F. Spread the 200 g sesame seeds on a sheet pan in a single layer. Toast for 10 to 12 minutes, shaking the pan every 3 minutes, until the seeds are pale gold and fragrant. Do NOT brown deeply ; the paste should stay light. The line between properly toasted and over-toasted is narrow; pull at the first hint of nutty aroma.'
    time_min: 12
  - text: 'Stub: cool the seeds completely on the pan, ~10 minutes. Transfer to a food processor or high-speed blender. Process for 2 minutes; the seeds will break down to a coarse meal.'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Stub: stream in the 50 g neutral oil with the processor running, then continue processing 6 to 8 minutes total, scraping the sides every 2 minutes. The mixture goes from coarse meal to thick paste to (eventually) smooth pourable tahini. Patience here ; the smoothness develops only at the end of the process.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Stub: add the optional 2 g fine sea salt, pulse to incorporate. Transfer to a clean jar. Refrigerate; the tahini holds 6 to 9 months refrigerated, with the surface oil separating slightly over time (stir before each use).'
    time_min: 2
