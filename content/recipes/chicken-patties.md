before_you_start:
  - 'Thaw the puff pastry overnight in the fridge, or 30 to 40 minutes on the counter wrapped in plastic-free parchment. Pastry must be cold and pliable but not frozen when rolled; frozen pastry cracks, warm pastry tears, and either way the seal fails and filling escapes during the bake.'
  - 'The keema filling must be at room temperature before it goes into the pastry, about 15 to 20 minutes spread on a wide plate. Warm filling melts the butter in the puff pastry layers and the patties bake to a flat, dense, wrong-textured pastry instead of the high-puff shatter that defines them.'
  - 'Make the keema first, then prep the pastry while the keema cools. The two timelines align: the 20 minute cool window is exactly when you roll, cut, and stage the squares.'

steps:
  - text: 'Make the keema filling. Heat the 30 ml oil in a wide skillet over medium heat. Add the 3 g cumin seeds; bloom 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the 150 g chopped onion; stir-fry 6 to 8 minutes until deep golden-brown. The browning is structural to flavor depth.'
    technique: 'maillard-sear'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Add aromatics and brown the chicken. Stir in the 25 g ginger-garlic paste and 8 g green chilies; stir-fry 60 seconds until the raw garlic note goes off. Add the 400 g ground chicken; break up with a spatula and stir-fry 5 to 6 minutes until the chicken is no longer pink, dry, and starting to brown at the edges. Use the spatula to break up any clumps; the keema texture is fine granular, like the pebbly texture of well-cooked taco meat.'
    time_min: 6
  - text: 'Add spices and tomato. Stir in the 6 g ground coriander, 4 g Kashmiri chili powder, 1 g turmeric, 6 g salt, and 2 g black pepper. Stir-fry 30 to 45 seconds until the spices coat the chicken and the kitchen fills with the aroma. Add the 100 g chopped tomato. Stir-fry 4 to 5 minutes; the tomato will release water, then break down, and the keema will become moist-cohesive (not wet, but not dry either). Pull when most of the visible liquid has cooked off.'
    technique: 'red-oil-rendering'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Finish the filling. Stir in the 4 g garam masala, 15 g chopped cilantro, and 8 ml lemon juice. Stir 30 seconds off heat. Taste; adjust salt. Spread the keema on a wide plate to cool to room temperature, about 15 to 20 minutes. The filling must be cool before it goes into pastry; warm filling melts the butter in the puff pastry and produces wrong-textured patties.'
    time_min: 18
  - text: 'Roll out the pastry. Flour a clean countertop lightly. Unfold one sheet of thawed puff pastry (250 g; 1 sheet of the 500 g / 2-sheet package). Roll gently with a rolling pin to a 30 cm × 30 cm square (about 3 mm thick). Cut into 4 equal squares (each 15 cm × 15 cm). Repeat with the second 250 g sheet. You should have 8 squares total.'
    time_min: 6
  - text: 'Fill and seal the patties. Place 2 to 2½ tbsp (about 50 g) of cooled keema filling in the center of each square, leaving a 2 cm border around the filling. Brush the borders lightly with beaten egg wash. Fold each square diagonally to make a triangle, pressing the edges firmly to seal. Crimp the edges with a fork tine for a decorative seal that also reinforces the closure. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving 3 cm between patties (they expand during baking).'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Egg wash and bake. Brush the tops of each patty with the beaten egg wash. Sprinkle with the 4 g / 2 tsp sesame seeds and 2 g / 1 tsp nigella seeds (if using). Bake in a preheated 200°C / 400°F oven for 22 to 25 minutes, until the patties are deeply golden-brown, the pastry is fully puffed, and the bottoms are crisp when lifted with a spatula. The internal temperature of the filling should reach at least 75°C / 165°F (the chicken was already cooked through in the keema phase, but the temperature check confirms food safety). Cool 5 minutes on the baking sheet, then transfer to a wire rack to finish cooling slightly.'
    time_min: 25
  - text: 'Serve. Serve warm or at room temperature. Patties are eaten by hand, often with a cup of strong masala chai or sweet milky tea. Tomato ketchup or a green chutney (mint-cilantro) is the typical accompaniment; some Pakistani households serve with sliced raw onion and a wedge of lemon. The patty should give a faint crunch on first bite, then yield to the soft spiced filling inside.'
    time_min: 0

notes: |
  Why thigh meat for keema. Ground chicken thigh has more fat (around 8% vs 2% for breast) and significantly more flavor. The keema cooked with thigh is moist, savory, and self-bastes; the same recipe with ground breast produces dry, bland filling that needs additional fat to compensate. If only breast meat is available, add 30 g extra oil (or better, 30 g ghee) to the cooking; the dish reads slightly different but works.

  The pastry-handling rule. Puff pastry is structurally fragile when warm: the butter layers melt at room temperature and the laminated structure collapses. Work cold: thaw pastry in the fridge overnight, work on a cool counter, fill and seal quickly, refrigerate the assembled patties 10 minutes before baking if the kitchen is warm. The result is patties that puff dramatically in the oven; pastry that has gone too warm produces patties that puff sluggishly and have visibly less layered structure.

  Why the cool-the-filling rule is non-negotiable. Warm keema filling placed into puff pastry melts the butter layers from the inside, producing a soggy bottom and underwhelming puff. Cool the filling completely (room temperature, 20+ minutes on a wide plate) before filling the patties. If pressed for time, spread the keema thin on a baking sheet and refrigerate 10 minutes; this works to bring the temperature down quickly.

  Eating mechanics. Patties are tea-snack food in Pakistani culture: served with strong masala chai, often as part of an afternoon spread that includes samosas, pakoras, mixed nuts, and biscuits. School lunchboxes; office tea breaks; family gatherings. The patties keep at room temperature 2 to 3 hours after baking; refrigerate any leftovers up to 3 days. Reheat in a 175°C / 350°F oven for 8 to 10 minutes (microwave reheating produces soggy pastry — avoid). Freeze unbaked assembled patties for up to 1 month; bake from frozen at 200°C / 400°F for 28 to 32 minutes.
