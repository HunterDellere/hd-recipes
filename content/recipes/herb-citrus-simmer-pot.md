  - text: 'Add the 1500 ml / 6 cups water, everything submerged with 5 cm / 2 inches headspace. Bring to a bare simmer over medium heat, then drop to the lowest setting on your stove. notes: |
  This pot is the deodorizer rather than the perfume. Heavy cooking odors, fish, fried food, garlic, onion, settle into fabrics and linger for hours. Running this simmer pot for 2 to 3 hours after a heavy cooking session displaces those persistent odors with fresh volatile compounds (limonene from citrus, menthol from mint, eugenol from basil) that the nose reads as cleansing. More effective than candles or air fresheners which simply mask underneath.

  Fresh herbs lose aromatic oils within 6 to 8 hours of sustained heat. The pot smells strongest in the first 2 hours, useful for the next 4, and washed out by hour 8. This is the trade-off for using fresh ingredients; spice-based pots run longer because dried spices release oils more slowly. Refresh fresh herbs at hour 6 to extend, or accept the natural shorter run time.

  Lemongrass is the secret ingredient. Even a single bruised stalk adds a rounded floral-citrus undertone that distinguishes a thoughtfully built herb pot from a generic citrus-mint pot. If you have access to fresh lemongrass, never skip it; if you have a freezer, keep extras frozen, bruising and slicing works from frozen with no loss.
