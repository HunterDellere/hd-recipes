steps:
  - text: 'Heat the oven to 200 °C / 400 °F. Pour the 500 g / 2 cups kosher salt into a small heavy roasting pan or cast-iron skillet to make a 1 cm bed; nestle the 700 g / 1.5 lb beets in the salt skin-on, root-up, and tuck the 4 thyme sprigs around them. The salt bed conducts heat evenly to the underside of each beet, draws surface moisture out (which concentrates the geosmin and earthy-sweet beet sugars), and prevents the dripping beet juice from scorching the pan.'
    technique: 'salt-roasting'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Roast 60 to 75 minutes until a paring knife slides into the centre of the largest beet with no resistance. Salt-bed roasting runs slightly slower than foil-wrapped roasting because the salt is a poor conductor of radiative heat; the trade is a more concentrated finished beet. Pull the pan, let the beets rest in the salt bed for 10 minutes; the residual heat completes the cook and the skins loosen.'
    time_min: 75
  - text: 'While the beets roast, build the pistachio crumble. Heat a dry skillet over medium and toast the 60 g / ½ cup pistachios for 4 to 5 minutes, shaking constantly, until the nuts smell intensely green-floral and the pale skin shows light gold edges. Pull off heat the moment the smell turns; pistachios go from toasted to scorched in 30 seconds and the chlorophyll-rich green will turn bitter brown if pushed.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Cool the pistachios 5 minutes on a plate (they crisp as they cool), then chop coarsely on a board. Toss with the 3 g / 1 tsp urfa biber, the 8 g / 2 tsp olive oil, and the 1 g / ¼ tsp flaky salt. The oil binds the urfa flakes to the nut surfaces; the urfa is dried at low temperature and salt-cured, so its raisin-and-cocoa profile reads through warm fat without needing a separate bloom.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Build the whipped feta in a food processor. Add the 200 g / 7 oz feta in chunks and pulse 8 to 10 times to break it down. Add the 100 g Greek yogurt, the 30 g / 2 tbsp olive oil, the 10 g / 2 tsp lemon juice, and the 2 g lemon zest. Process 60 to 90 seconds, stopping once to scrape, until the texture is smooth and spreading-soft like room-temperature ricotta. The yogurt is the lubricator; the olive oil makes it glossy; lemon brightens what is already very salty cheese. Taste, then adjust: feta varies, and if your batch is mild, add another 1 g / pinch of fine sea salt.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'When the beets are cool enough to handle but still warm, slip the skins off under a paper towel; they slide off in sheets. Cut each beet into wedges or 1 cm slabs. While still warm, dress them in a wide bowl with the 15 g / 1 tbsp red wine vinegar, the 15 g / 1 tbsp olive oil, and the 2 g / ½ tsp flaky salt. Warm beets absorb the vinaigrette into their cellular structure rather than letting it pool; this is why the timing matters.'
    technique: 'vinaigrette-emulsion'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Supreme the 400 g / 2 blood oranges. Cut off both poles to expose the flesh, stand the fruit on one cut end, and slice the peel-and-pith away in curved strips down to the flesh. Hold the trimmed fruit over a bowl and slice between each membrane to release the segments; the segments fall into the bowl, leaving a fan of empty membranes. Squeeze the membranes over the bowl to capture the residual juice — this juice goes back into the salad as a finishing acid, not down the sink.'
    time_min: 6
  - text: 'Spread the whipped feta in a wide swoosh across a large platter or shallow bowl, leaving the lip clean. Use the back of a spoon to push the feta into a gentle craters-and-ridges landscape; flat whipped feta wastes the surface area that catches juices.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Compose, do not toss. Lay the warm dressed beets across the feta. Tuck the blood-orange segments around the beets so the colors alternate (the deep crimson against the magenta-streaked orange is the visual point of the dish). Spoon a tablespoon of the reserved blood-orange juice over the whole platter.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Scatter the urfa-pistachio crumble across the top in a wide arc, then drape the 6 g torn mint leaves over the platter. Drizzle the 10 g / 2 tsp honey from a spoon held high, in thin streams that catch on the beets and the cheese rather than pooling. Serve at room temperature within 20 minutes; the warm beet against the cool feta is the textural argument.'
    time_min: 2

notes: |
  Why salt-roast rather than foil-wrap: the salt bed pulls surface moisture out of the beet during the cook, which concentrates the cellular sugars and the geosmin (the compound responsible for the earthy beet character). Foil-wrapped beets steam as much as they roast and retain more water; the finished beet is paler in flavor. The same salt bed reuses across 8 to 10 roasts before the salt picks up enough beet juice to turn pink and flat-flavored; pour it back into a jar after each use.

  Why whipped feta rather than crumbled: crumbled feta scattered over a beet salad makes every bite taste of feta-and-beet in alternation. Whipped feta as a base layer means every bite reads of feta + beet + citrus simultaneously; the cheese is the dressing, not a topping. The yogurt is structural — pure whipped feta is too dense and salt-aggressive; the dilution to a 2:1 cheese:yogurt ratio lands in the spreading texture window.

  The umami source is the feta itself (salt-fermented sheep cheese, intensely glutamate-rich); the finishing acid is the blood orange juice and the lemon in the whipped feta. Both axes are baked in, not added at the end.

  Compose at room temperature, not chilled. Cold beets read flat (the geosmin volatiles are temperature-dependent), cold whipped feta is gummy. Build the salad within 30 minutes of slicing the beets; the dish loses its warm-on-cool textural argument once the beets equilibrate downward.
