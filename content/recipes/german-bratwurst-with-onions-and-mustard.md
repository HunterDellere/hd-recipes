  note: 'Bratwurst is the structural German fresh pork sausage; Nürnberger, Thüringer, and Weisswurst are regional variants. The structural cooking method is poach-then-sear: the gentle poach (in beer, water, or beer-and-onion broth) cooks the filling through without bursting the natural casing; the hot iron sear crisps the casing into the snap that defines a properly cooked brat. The Bavarian biergarten serves brats with sweet German mustard (süßer Senf) on a soft pretzel roll or a slice of dark rye; the Wisconsin-German variant serves them on a hot dog bun with caramelized onions, sauerkraut, and a stein of German lager.'

before_you_start:
  - 'Fresh raw bratwurst (not pre-cooked) is the structural Bavarian preparation. American "Johnsonville" supermarket brats are fresh and acceptable; German-grocery or German-butcher brats are better. Avoid pre-cooked or smoked sausages labeled "brat" (different category entirely); these can be grilled hot directly without the poach.'
  - 'The poach water must stay below 80 °C / 175 °F (a bare simmer with single bubbles rising lazily). Boiling at 100 °C bursts the casings within 30 seconds. A thermometer in the pot is useful; without one, the visual cue is "tremor, not boil."'
  - 'The caramelized onions take 25 to 30 minutes of patient cooking at medium-low heat. Start them at the same time as the brat poach so they finish together. Rushed onions taste underdone and harshly oniony rather than sweet-deep.'

steps:
  - text: 'Start the brat poach. Combine the 500 ml beer with 500 ml water in a wide deep saucepan or skillet (the brats should lie flat in a single layer). Add the 200 g sliced onion rings, 2 bay leaves, and 2 g black peppercorns. Bring to 75 °C / 165 °F over medium heat; the surface should show only occasional bubbles, not a boil.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Lower the 4 bratwurst gently into the warm beer-onion broth. The brats should be just submerged; add more water if needed. Maintain the temperature at 75 to 80 °C; if the surface starts to boil, reduce heat or pull off briefly. Poach 20 minutes; the brats are cooked through when they read 70 °C / 160 °F internal (instant-read thermometer in the thickest part).'
    time_min: 20
  - text: 'While the brats poach, start the caramelized onions in a wide skillet. Melt the 30 g / 2 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add the 400 g sliced onions and 3 g salt; stir to coat. Reduce heat to medium-low. Cook 25 to 30 minutes, stirring every 4 to 5 minutes, until the onions have reduced to about a third of their starting volume, turned deep golden-brown, and smell aggressively sweet. Do not crank the heat; brown but-not-burnt slow caramelization is the structural target.'
    time_min: 30
  - text: 'When the brats are cooked through (after 20 min poach), lift them out with tongs to a paper-towel-lined plate. Pat dry; surface water prevents the sear. Discard the poach liquid (or reserve a splash for warming the sauerkraut).'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Heat a cast iron or heavy stainless skillet over medium-high heat for 3 minutes until very hot. Add the 15 ml / 1 tbsp neutral oil. Lay the poached brats in the hot skillet. Sear 90 seconds per side (4 sides total, rotating to brown evenly), for a total sear time of 5 to 6 minutes, until the casings are deep mahogany and have crisped to a structural snap. The casings should crackle audibly when you press them with the tongs; this is the structural sear that defines a proper German brat.'
    technique: 'maillard-sear'
    time_min: 6
  - text: 'While the brats sear, warm the 200 g sauerkraut in a small pan over low heat with a splash (30 ml) of the reserved poach liquid for 3 to 4 minutes.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Plate. Split each pretzel roll or hot dog bun. Spread 15 g sweet German mustard on the inside of the roll. Place a hot seared brat in the roll. Top with a generous spoon of caramelized onions and a side of sauerkraut. Serve with a German-style potato salad, fresh radishes, and a stein of cold German lager.'
    time_min: 3

notes: |
  Why poach then sear. The poach (75 to 80 °C, 20 min) gently cooks the bratwurst filling through to a fully-cooked 70 °C internal temperature without stressing the natural hog casing. The sear (high-heat iron skillet, 90 sec per side) crisps the casing into the snap that is the structural signature of a properly cooked German brat. Skipping the poach and grilling raw brats hot risks bursting the casing before the inside cooks; the resulting brat is dry inside and burned outside. Skipping the sear and serving the poached brat alone produces a pale flabby brat without the structural casing snap.

  Why the casing snap. Natural hog casing is collagen-and-protein; under high dry heat, the collagen tightens and the surface dehydrates into a rigid crispy skin that audibly snaps when bitten. This snap is the structural sensory event of eating a bratwurst; without it, the brat reads as soft and boring. The poach softens the casing; the sear crisps it back.

  Why the beer-onion poach. The beer adds malt-and-hop aromatics to the brat through the casing; the onions add sweetness and depth. Plain water poach is acceptable but produces a less flavorful brat. Some Wisconsin households "swim" their brats in beer for hours before grilling; this is the brewery-tavern tradition.

  The sweet-mustard rule. Sweet Bavarian mustard (süßer Senf) is the structural Bavarian condiment for white sausages (Weisswurst) and a popular pairing for bratwurst. The sweet-tangy profile (from sugar, vinegar, brown mustard seeds) is fundamentally different from American yellow mustard (mild, vinegar-forward) or French Dijon (sharp, wine-vinegar-forward). Substituting the wrong mustard pulls the dish out of its German frame.

  Pairings. Canonical with German-style potato salad (warm, vinegar-based, with bacon and mustard), sauerkraut, and a German lager. Pretzel rolls (Laugenbrötchen) are the Bavarian bread; American hot dog buns are the Wisconsin substitute. Radishes (sliced thin and salted) are the Bavarian biergarten side.

  Storage. Cooked brats keep 3 days refrigerated; reheat in a covered pan over low heat or in a 175 °C / 350 °F oven 8 minutes. Caramelized onions keep 1 week refrigerated and improve overnight.
