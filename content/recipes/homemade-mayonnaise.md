steps:
  - text: 'Stub: immersion-blender route. Combine the 1 whole egg, 5 g Dijon, 15 ml lemon juice, and 3 g salt in the bottom of a tall narrow vessel (a 2-cup measuring cup or the cup that came with the immersion blender). Pour the 250 ml oil on top of the egg mixture; let settle. Plunge the immersion blender to the bottom, turn on, and hold at the bottom for 15 seconds, the mayo will form at the base. Slowly lift the blender as the emulsion builds, taking 60 to 75 seconds total to incorporate all the oil. The mayo should be thick, glossy, pale yellow, and stand in soft peaks.'
    time_min: 2
  - text: 'Stub: hand-whisk route (the classical French method). Combine the 2 egg yolks, 5 g Dijon, 5 ml of the lemon juice, and 3 g salt in a medium bowl set on a damp tea towel (the towel keeps the bowl from sliding). Whisk briefly to combine. Drizzle in the 250 ml oil drop by drop at the start, whisking continuously, until the emulsion has formed (about the first 30 ml of oil). Then add the remaining oil in a slow steady stream, continuing to whisk vigorously, taking 5 to 7 minutes total. Whisk in the remaining lemon juice at the end. The mayo will be slightly looser than the immersion-blender version; both are correct.'
    time_min: 7
  - text: 'Stub: taste and adjust. The mayo should taste eggy, lemony, mustardy, salty, with a clean neutral oil background. If flat, add salt. If too thick, whisk in a few drops of water. If broken (separated): start a new yolk in a new bowl, then drizzle the broken mayo into the new yolk while whisking, the new yolk pulls the broken emulsion back into a stable mayo. Refrigerate 15 minutes before use; flavors integrate during the rest.'
    time_min: 1
