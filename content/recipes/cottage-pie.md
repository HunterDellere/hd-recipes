  note: 'Cottage pie (the beef version) and shepherds pie (the lamb version) are structural sibling dishes in British home cooking, emerging in the 19th century as ways to use leftover roast meat. The modern home version uses fresh ground meat braised with vegetables in a thick gravy, topped with mashed potato, and baked. The naming rule is strict: shepherds pie is lamb (a shepherd tends sheep); cottage pie is beef (a cottager has a cow). Calling a beef pie a shepherds pie is the most common British-food error in American home kitchens.'

before_you_start:
  - 'The mashed potato topping needs to be made fresh and used hot; cold mashed potato is hard to spread evenly over the beef and produces a clumpy crust. Time the mash to finish at the same moment as the beef braise reduces.'
  - 'The beef should braise down for at least 40 minutes after the liquid goes in. A 15 minute fast simmer produces a watery filling that soaks into the potato crust during the bake; the structural cottage pie has a thick gravy that holds its shape under the potato.'

steps:
  - text: 'Preheat oven to 200 °C / 400 °F. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the potatoes; add about 6 g of the kosher salt.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Heat the 30 ml / 2 tbsp neutral oil in a wide deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add the 200 g diced onion, 150 g diced carrot, and 80 g diced celery; sauté 7 to 8 minutes until softened with light golden edges.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Add the 15 g / 4 cloves garlic and 4 g / 2 tsp thyme; cook 60 seconds until fragrant.'
    technique: 'blooming-aromatics'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Push the vegetables to one side of the pan. Add the 700 g ground beef to the cleared space. Break up with a wooden spoon and cook 6 to 7 minutes, mostly undisturbed, until the beef has lost all pink and the bottom of the pan has developed a brown fond. Stir the beef into the vegetables.'
    technique: 'maillard-sear'
    time_min: 7
  - text: 'Add the 30 g / 2 tbsp tomato paste; cook 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste has darkened from bright red to brick red. Add the 15 g / 2 tbsp flour; stir 60 seconds to cook out the raw flour taste and start the roux.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Pour in the 150 ml / ⅔ cup red wine; scrape the fond off the pan bottom. Cook 90 seconds to reduce by half and burn off the alcohol. Add the 400 ml / 1⅔ cups beef stock, 15 ml / 1 tbsp Worcestershire, 8 g / 2 tsp salt, and 3 g / 1½ tsp pepper. Bring to a steady simmer; reduce heat to medium-low and cook 35 to 40 minutes, partially covered, stirring occasionally, until the braise has thickened into a glossy gravy that coats a spoon and the liquid level has dropped to just below the meat.'
    technique: 'deglazing'
    time_min: 40
  - text: 'While the beef braises, add the 1000 g potato chunks to the salted boiling water. Cook 18 to 22 minutes until a knife slides through with no resistance. Drain well in a colander; let the steam evaporate for 60 seconds (excess water in the potato makes the mash gluey).'
    time_min: 22
  - text: 'Return the drained potatoes to the empty hot pot. Add the 90 g / 6 tbsp butter; mash with a potato masher until the butter has melted into the mash. Pour in the 120 ml / ½ cup warm milk and 60 ml / ¼ cup cream; mash to a smooth thick consistency. Season with the remaining 8 g / 2 tsp salt and 2 g / 1 tsp pepper. Taste; adjust seasoning.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Stir the 100 g frozen peas into the finished beef braise; cook 2 minutes to warm through. Transfer the beef braise to a 25 cm round or 20 × 30 cm rectangular baking dish; spread evenly to about 3 cm thick.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Spoon the hot mashed potato over the beef in dollops. Spread gently with the back of a spoon to cover the surface fully, sealing right to the edges of the dish (the seal prevents the gravy from bubbling up around the edges). Use a fork to score the surface in ridges and peaks; the ridges crisp into deep gold during the bake. If using, sprinkle the 50 g grated cheddar over the top.'
    time_min: 5
  - text: 'Bake at 200 °C / 400 °F for 25 to 30 minutes, until the potato crust is deep gold with crispy ridges and the beef braise is bubbling around the edges. If the top is not browning enough, finish under the broiler for 90 seconds, watching closely. Rest 10 minutes before serving; the rest lets the gravy thicken and the pie hold its shape when scooped.'
    time_min: 30

notes: |
  Why thick gravy is structural. Cottage pie is engineered to hold its shape when scooped onto a plate, with the potato crust intact on top and the beef gravy thick enough to stay where it is rather than running. The 35 to 40 minute braise reduces the liquid to a glossy thick gravy; a watery braise produces a pie where the potato sinks during the bake and the slices fall apart when served.

  Why fork-scored ridges on the mash. The ridges produced by dragging a fork across the surface of the mash crisp into deep golden peaks during the bake, while the troughs between the ridges stay softer. The textural contrast between crispy peaks and creamy underneath is the structural pleasure of a proper cottage pie crust. A perfectly smooth mash crust is a missed opportunity.

  The cheddar question. Cheese on top of cottage pie (or shepherds pie) is a 20th-century home addition, not the historical canonical version. Most British home cooks add it; most British pub kitchens do not. The cheesy crust reads slightly more comforting and home-style; the no-cheese crust reads slightly more traditional and clean. Either is fine; the choice is taste.

  Pairings. A side of buttered peas (if you have not folded them into the filling), or a green salad, or steamed broccoli. A pint of British bitter or a hearty red wine. Brown sauce (HP or Daddies) at the table is the British pub addition; ketchup is American.

  Storage. Reheats beautifully; if anything, the dish improves overnight as the flavors integrate. Keeps 4 days refrigerated. Reheat at 180 °C / 350 °F covered for 25 minutes, then uncovered 10 more to re-crisp the top. Freezes well; freeze either before baking (assemble, freeze in dish, bake from frozen at 180 °C / 350 °F for 60 minutes) or after baking (cool, slice, freeze in portions, reheat 25 minutes covered in oven).
