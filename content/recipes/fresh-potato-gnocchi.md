steps:
  - text: 'Stub: bake the 1000 g russets whole at 200 °C for 60 to 75 minutes until a knife slides through with no resistance. Baking (rather than boiling) is structural, boiling waterlogs the potato and the dumplings absorb water on cooking and turn gummy. The bake-vs-boil decision is the single largest variable in gnocchi quality.'
    time_min: 75
  - text: 'Stub: while the potatoes are still hot (this matters; cold potato builds gluten differently), peel and pass through a ricer or food mill onto a clean work surface. Spread thin, sprinkle with the 6 g salt, and let cool for 5 minutes. The ricer produces the fluffy, no-gluten texture; a food processor or mashing collapses the starch and produces a gummy dough that will not work.'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Stub: form a well in the riced potato. Add the 18 g egg yolk and 30 g grated parmigiano if using. Sprinkle the 200 g flour over the surface in two or three stages, working the dough gently with a bench scraper to fold flour in. Stop adding flour the moment the dough holds together when squeezed; resist the home-cook instinct to add more flour. The dough should feel slightly tacky but not sticky.'
    time_min: 8
  - text: 'Stub: divide the dough into 6 portions. Roll each portion into a 2 cm rope on a lightly floured surface. Cut the rope into 2 cm pillows with a sharp knife or bench scraper. Optionally roll each pillow over a gnocchi paddle or the back of a fork tine to create the ridges that will hold sauce. Place formed gnocchi on a flour-dusted parchment-lined sheet in a single layer; do not stack.'
    time_min: 15
  - text: 'Stub: bring the 4 L water and 25 g salt to a rolling boil. Drop the gnocchi in batches of 30 to 40 (do not crowd; the water temperature drops with each addition and crowded gnocchi stick to each other). Cook 90 seconds past the moment they float to the surface; lift out with a spider strainer; transfer directly to the awaiting sauce. Do not drain in a colander, the residual cooking water carries starch that helps the sauce cling.'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Stub: serve immediately. Gnocchi do not hold; even 5 minutes of sitting in a warm sauce produces a softer, less distinct dumpling. Plate and eat fast.'
