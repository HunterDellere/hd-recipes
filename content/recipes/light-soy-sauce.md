steps:
  - text: 'Stub: prepare the koji-cultured grain. Soak 1500 g / 3.3 lb soybeans 12 hours, drain, pressure-cook 60 minutes until the beans crush easily between fingers. Toast 750 g / 1.65 lb wheat berries in a dry skillet 5 minutes until fragrant; pulse-grind in a food processor to a coarse cracked-grain texture. Combine cooled cooked soybeans and cracked wheat at body temperature (35 to 37 °C); inoculate with 5 g / 1 tsp koji starter spores. Spread on shallow trays and incubate at 30 °C / 86 °F with 80 to 90 percent humidity for 48 to 72 hours; stir twice daily. The substrate develops a white-to-pale-green mold coverage and smells distinctly of fresh-mushroom and chestnut.'
    time_min: 4320
  - text: 'Stub: prepare the moromi (the brine fermentation). Combine the koji-cultured grain with 600 g / 2½ cups kosher salt and 3000 ml / 12 cups filtered water in a wide-mouth fermentation crock or food-grade plastic bucket; the salt-to-water ratio is approximately 18 to 20 percent salinity. Stir vigorously to break up clumps. Cover with a clean cloth and a fitted lid that allows gas escape (a fermentation airlock or a loose lid weighted with a clean weight). Place in a stable 25 to 30 °C location.'
    time_min: 30
  - text: 'Stub: ferment for 6 to 18 months. Stir vigorously once weekly for the first month, then once monthly for the duration. The moromi develops a complex aromatic profile through the fermentation: month 1 to 3 reads as raw-koji and harshly salty; month 6 reads as recognizably soy-sauce-like; month 12 reads as deep and complex. The longer the ferment, the deeper and more nuanced the finished sauce.'
    time_min: 257040
  - text: 'Stub: press and pasteurize. After the desired ferment time, transfer the moromi to a press cloth (cheesecloth-lined colander suspended over a stockpot, or a dedicated fruit press) and squeeze out all liquid. The press yield is approximately 1500 to 1800 ml of raw soy sauce. Pasteurize at 70 to 80 °C / 160 to 175 °F for 30 minutes (this halts the fermentation, kills any pathogenic microorganisms, and develops the soy sauce''s final aromatic profile through Maillard reactions during the heat treatment). Bottle in clean glass; the pasteurized sauce is shelf-stable indefinitely refrigerated.'
    time_min: 30
