  Hydration adjustment: 72% hydration is moderate for a country sourdough; some flours absorb less and produce a slack dough at this percentage. Reduce to 70% if the dough feels unmanageable, increase to 75% if the dough feels stiff. Whole wheat flour absorbs more water than bread flour, so the 15% whole wheat in this recipe pulls the effective hydration down slightly.

  Starter assumption: this stub assumes the cook has a mature, well-fed sourdough starter on hand. Building a new starter from scratch takes 7-14 days and is a separate project. Once a starter is established, this recipe is a 24-hour cycle from levain build to baked loaf.
