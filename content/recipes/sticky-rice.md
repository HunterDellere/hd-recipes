Submerge in 8 cups of cold water in a bowl, cover, and let soak overnight at room temperature (8 to 12 hours minimum). The grains will swell to roughly 1.5x their original volume and turn from opaque white to translucent.'
    time_min: 480
  - text: 'Stub: drain the soaked rice. Set up the steaming rig: a bamboo basket (huat) over a deep pot of boiling water, with the basket rim sitting clear of the water. Spread the soaked rice in the basket in an even layer. Steam over high heat for 20 to 25 minutes, flipping the rice mound at the 12-minute mark (lift the basket off, invert the rice onto a plate, then return to the basket so the bottom rice goes to the top). The rice is done when it is uniformly translucent, sticky-but-not-mushy, and chewable without grit.'
