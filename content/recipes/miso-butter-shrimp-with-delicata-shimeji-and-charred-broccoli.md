steps:
  - text: 'Heat the oven to 230°C / 450°F with a heavy rimmed sheet pan on the middle rack. The pan must be ripping hot before the squash hits it; a cold pan steams the squash; a 230°C pan gets you Maillard browning in 90 seconds.'
    time_min: 12

  - text: 'Brine the shrimp. Toss 680 g / 1.5 lb shrimp with 4 g / ¾ tsp baking soda and 4 g / ¾ tsp kosher salt in a bowl. Let sit at room temperature while you prep the vegetables. The alkaline-saline cure shifts surface protein structure and locks in water; the shrimp will read snappier and juicier with no extra effort.'
    time_min: 15

  - text: 'In a large bowl, toss 600 g / 1.3 lb delicata half-moons with 15 g / 1 tbsp avocado oil and 3 g / ½ tsp of the kosher salt. Spread on the preheated sheet pan in a single layer. Roast 12 minutes; do not stir. The flat sides need full pan contact to char; flipping early means pale, soft squash.'
    time_min: 12

  - text: 'While the squash roasts, build the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk 45 g / 3 tbsp white miso, 30 g / 2 tbsp mirin, 30 g / 2 tbsp sake, 15 g / 1 tbsp light soy sauce, 12 g / 1 tbsp grated ginger, and 12 g / 3 cloves grated garlic. The miso will fight you for ten seconds, then loosen; keep whisking. Set aside.'
    time_min: 3

  - text: 'In the same vegetable bowl, toss 450 g / 1 lb broccoli (florets and bias-cut stems) and 200 g / 2 packs separated shimeji with the remaining 15 g / 1 tbsp avocado oil and 2 g / ½ tsp kosher salt. Do not crowd them; if the bowl looks soggy, blot dry with a towel before they hit the pan.'
    time_min: 2

  - text: 'Pull the squash. Push it to one half of the sheet pan, scatter the 450 g / 1 lb broccoli and 200 g / 7 oz shimeji in the open half, separating the shimeji into a single layer. The shimeji must touch the pan directly; they are dry-searing first, picking up oil from the pan film, not sitting in oil. Roast 10 minutes. The broccoli florets should char at the tips; the shimeji should crisp at the cap edges and stop reading bitter.'
    time_min: 10

  - text: 'Melt 60 g / 4 tbsp butter in a small saucepan over medium-low. Cook past the foaming stage until the milk solids smell faintly toasted; about 90 seconds, stop before deep-amber browning (we want some Maillard depth, not full beurre noisette here, because the miso has its own toasted backbone).'
    technique: 'brown-butter'
    time_min: 2

  - text: 'Off heat, whisk the miso mixture into the warm 60 g / 4 tbsp browned butter. The glaze will look glossy and slightly broken; this is fine; the emulsion reforms when it hits the hot vegetables.'
    time_min: 1

  - text: 'Pull the sheet pan. Push everything to one side. Pat the brined shrimp dry; surface moisture is the enemy of a sear; and spread them in a single layer on the empty half of the pan. Brush the shrimp with about a third of the glaze (~50 g). Return to the oven, switch to broil on high (top rack about 15 cm from the element), and broil 3–4 minutes until the shrimp curl into Cs (not Os; Os mean overcooked) and the tails turn deep coral with charred edges. Pull immediately at first sign of color set; carryover does the rest.'
    time_min: 4

  - text: 'Drizzle the remaining glaze over the vegetables on the pan, then drizzle 10 g / 2 tsp rice vinegar across the entire pan. Toss with a spatula directly on the sheet pan; the residual heat tightens the glaze into a coat instead of a puddle. The vinegar cuts the salt-sweet weight of the miso-butter and pulls the dish together; without it the plate reads heavy and one-note.'
    time_min: 1

  - text: 'Finish with 5 g / 1 tsp toasted sesame oil drizzled across, then scatter 30 g / 3 sliced scallion greens and 6 g / 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds. Serve directly from the sheet pan or transfer to a warmed platter. Pair with short-grain rice.'
    time_min: 1

Don''t broil scallops; the dry-heat sear is what they want.'

    how: 'Replace the white miso with 30 g / 2 tbsp gochujang and 15 g / 1 tbsp doenjang. Sake stays. Add 2 g / 1 tsp gochugaru to the vegetable toss. Finish with perilla oil instead of sesame, and a squeeze of yuzu or lime instead of rice vinegar. The dish moves from washoku-restraint to fermented-funk-and-fruit-heat.'

notes: |
  This recipe is engineered around two heat windows and a finishing acid.

  **The vegetable window** is dry, hard, and uninterrupted. Delicata gets a head start because winter squash is dense and needs the longest. Broccoli and shimeji come in at minute 12 because they're fragile; broccoli florets char in 8 minutes, shimeji crisp in 6, and either of them on the pan from the beginning would turn to mush. The shimeji specifically need single-layer pan contact for the bitterness compounds (fasciculol) to break down; pile them up and they'll read raw and acrid in the finished dish.

  **The shrimp window** is short and aggressive. Brining (alkaline + salt) at 0.6% baking soda by weight changes the surface charge of the shrimp protein, locking water in and producing a noticeably snappier bite. Broiling under high heat for 3–4 minutes lets the surface caramelize via the miso glaze without overcooking the interior; shrimp internal target is 60–63°C and broiling overshoots fast. Pull the shrimp at C-shape, not O-shape; carryover finishes them.

  **The acid is non-negotiable.** Miso, butter, mirin, and soy stack heavy savory weight. The 2 tsp of rice vinegar at the end, drizzled on the hot pan, is what makes the dish read clean instead of muddy; it cuts through the fat and brightens the squash sweetness. If you taste at the end and it reads "good but heavy," more vinegar is your move, not more salt.

  **MSG is welcome but not required.** A pinch (~1 g) added to the glaze during whisking deepens the umami without adding salt. Optional, but in line with the broader washoku flavor architecture.
