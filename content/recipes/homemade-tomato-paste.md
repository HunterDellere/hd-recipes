steps:
  - text: 'Heat oven to 95 °C / 200 °F with a rack in the middle. Pour the 2400 g canned tomatoes into a large bowl; crush by hand into rough chunks (do not blend; the texture matters less in the paste than in a sauce but blending creates too many fine particles that dry into a gritty texture). Spread in a thin, even layer over 2 heavy half-sheet pans (the surface area to volume ratio matters; deeper layers take longer to reduce and brown unevenly). Drizzle the 30 g / 2 tbsp olive oil over the tomatoes and sprinkle with the 3 g / ½ tsp salt.'
    time_min: 15
  - text: 'Roast 6 to 8 hours, stirring with a spatula every 30 to 45 minutes for the first 4 hours and every 15 to 20 minutes for the last 2 hours. The paste reduces visibly through the cook: hour 1, the tomatoes are still wet and chunky; hour 3, the texture is loose-sauce; hour 5, thick sauce, beginning to coat the pan; hour 7, paste-thick, deep rust-red, glossy, sticky. The endpoint is when a heaped spoonful holds its shape on a plate and the surface has caramelized to a deep brick-red color. Scrape any dried-paste edges into the center as you stir; the edges dry first and need to be reincorporated.'
    time_min: 420
  - text: 'Pack the finished paste into a clean 250 ml glass jar; press down to eliminate air pockets. Drizzle a thin layer of olive oil over the top (about 1 tbsp) to seal the surface against oxidation. Seal the jar tightly and refrigerate. The paste keeps 2 to 3 months refrigerated under the oil layer; once a portion is removed, top up the oil layer to keep the surface covered.'
    time_min: 5
