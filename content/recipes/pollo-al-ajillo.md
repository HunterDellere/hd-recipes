steps:
  - text: 'Salt the 1200 g chicken pieces with the 12 g / 1 tbsp salt and 2 g / 1 tsp black pepper, rubbing under and over the skin. Let rest uncovered on a rack in the refrigerator at least 30 minutes (or up to 24 hours; the dry-brine pulls moisture out of the skin and lets it crisp dramatically better during the sear).'
    time_min: 30
  - text: 'Heat the 150 ml / ⅔ cup olive oil in a wide heavy skillet or shallow Dutch oven (a 30 cm / 12 in cast iron is ideal) over medium-high heat until shimmering. Pat the chicken pieces dry with paper towels (the dry-brine has surfaced moisture; pat it off for a clean sear).'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Brown the chicken skin-side down in a single layer, undisturbed, for 7 to 9 minutes until the skin is deep mahogany and releases easily from the pan. Flip and brown the second side 4 to 5 minutes. The skin should be visibly crisp and the chicken just-cooked at the surface (it will not yet be cooked through). Remove the chicken to a plate.'
    technique: 'maillard-sear'
    time_min: 14
  - text: 'Reduce heat to medium-low. Add the 60 g / 1 head crushed-skin-on garlic cloves to the chicken-flavored oil. The cloves should sizzle gently but not bubble aggressively (if the oil is too hot, the garlic burns and the dish reads bitter; if too cool, the garlic stays raw and harsh). Cook the garlic 8 to 10 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes, until the cloves are uniformly deep golden, soft to a knife-poke, and the oil smells deeply of garlic. Add the 2 bay leaves, 3 g fresh thyme, and the 2 dried chiles in the last 2 minutes.'
    technique: 'blooming-aromatics'
    time_min: 10
  - text: 'Return the chicken to the pan, skin-side up, nestling it into the garlic-and-aromatic oil. The oil should come up about halfway on the chicken pieces (the structural oil-poach geometry). Reduce heat to low; cook gently uncovered for 12 to 15 minutes, basting the chicken with the hot oil every 4 minutes, until the chicken reaches 75°C / 165°F internal at the thickest part of the thigh. The skin stays crisp on top because it is exposed to air; the bottom of the chicken slowly oil-poaches at low temperature.'
    time_min: 15
  - text: 'Remove the chicken to a warm platter, skin-side up. Pour off all but 60 g / ¼ cup of the garlic-oil from the pan (reserve the poured-off oil; it is liquid gold for vegetables, eggs, bread). Leave the garlic cloves and aromatics in the pan.'
    time_min: 3
  - text: 'Pull the pan off heat briefly. Add the 3 g / 1 tsp sweet pimentón directly into the residual oil; stir 10 seconds in the residual heat to bloom (burnt paprika reads bitter, so do this off heat or on very low heat).'
    time_min: 1
  - text: 'Return to medium-high heat; immediately deglaze with the 180 ml / ¾ cup Fino sherry. It will steam and bubble aggressively. Scrape up any browned bits from the pan bottom. Reduce 3 to 4 minutes until the sherry has reduced by half and emulsified with the oil into a glossy spoonable sauce; the alcohol cooks off and the sherry concentrates into a savory glaze.'
    time_min: 4
  - text: 'Return the chicken (and any accumulated juices) to the pan; toss briefly to coat the chicken in the sherry-garlic-pimentón sauce. Scatter the 10 g chopped parsley over the top. Serve directly from the pan with crusty bread for mopping the sauce (essential), or over plain white rice. Pass the lemon wedges; a small squeeze on each piece lifts the dish. Eat the whole garlic cloves; squeeze them out of their skins onto the bread, they have transformed into sweet melted garlic paste.'
    time_min: 2

    kind: 'protein'
  - for: 'occasion'
    to: 'oven-finish version (more reliable for dinner parties)'
    how: 'Brown the chicken in steps 1 to 3 as written. Skip the stovetop oil-poach. After step 4 (garlic poach), nestle the chicken back into the garlic oil, transfer the whole pan to a 180°C / 350°F oven, and roast 20 to 25 minutes until 75°C / 165°F internal. The oven heat is gentler and more consistent than stovetop; less basting required. Finish with the pimentón-and-sherry deglaze on the stove as written.'
    kind: 'occasion'
  - for: 'cuisine pivot'
    to: 'pollo a la chilindrón (Aragonese version, with tomato and bell pepper)'
    how: 'After the garlic-oil poach in step 4, add 200 g chopped red bell pepper and 300 g canned San Marzano tomato; cook 8 minutes until pepper is tender and tomato has reduced. Return the chicken; braise 15 minutes covered. Skip the sherry deglaze (the tomato carries enough acid). Skip the pimentón (the dish reads pepper-forward without it). The dish is structurally distinct from ajillo; a Spanish-regional alternative for the same chicken cut.'
    kind: 'regional'

notes: |
  Why oil-poach instead of just sauté. The structural premise of pollo al ajillo is gentle cooking in garlic-infused oil, not aggressive frying. A 90°C to 110°C oil bath cooks the chicken through without driving moisture out of the meat; the result is dramatically more tender than chicken sautéed in a thin film of oil. The crisp top skin (exposed to air) and the gently-oil-poached underside is the structurally correct texture; the dish should read as "chicken cooked in olive oil" not "chicken fried in olive oil."

  Skin-on garlic cloves vs peeled. Skin-on garlic in the oil bath produces a sweeter, more mellow garlic flavor because the skin protects the cloves from direct contact with the hot oil and slows the Maillard browning to a gentler caramelization. Peeled garlic cloves brown faster and can edge into bitterness if not watched carefully. The traditional Spanish technique is skin-on. The skins slip off easily after cooking; eaters can pop the soft sweet cloves out of their skins onto bread.

  The sherry deglaze is the dish-defining moment. Fino sherry is structurally what makes this dish read as Spanish rather than French or Italian. The wine's bone-dry character, briny flor yeast notes, and almond aromatic deglaze the chicken fond, cut through the rich garlic oil, and concentrate into a glossy savory glaze that coats the chicken. White wine substitutes mechanically but the dish loses its Spanish signature; the sherry is structurally non-negotiable for the canonical version.

  Storage. Refrigerated in a sealed container, 3 to 4 days; the dish improves significantly overnight as the garlic-and-sherry sauce integrates into the chicken. Reheat gently in a covered pan over low heat with a splash of water or stock to loosen the sauce; do not microwave (the skin loses its crisp). The reserved garlic oil keeps refrigerated 2 weeks; use it for vegetables, fried eggs, bread crisps, or another batch of ajillo (cycle through it 2 to 3 times before discarding).
