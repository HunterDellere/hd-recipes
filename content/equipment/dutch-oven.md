about: |
  An enameled cast iron pot, 5–7 quart, with a heavy lid. The mass holds steady temperature for braising, the seal
  traps steam for moist cooking, and the depth handles stocks and stews without splashing. The defining feature is
  that it's stovetop-to-oven capable: a braise can sear on the stove and finish at 150°C in the oven without
  transferring to a second vessel, which preserves the fond and saves the dish from a temperature-drop step that
  pulls juices out of the meat.

  Spec that matters: 5.5qt is the sweet spot for 4-person braises — large enough for a 1.5kg shoulder with
  aromatics, small enough that the liquid doesn't spread thin. Weight runs 5–8kg; the heft is the trade for the
  thermal stability. Enamel interior means you can deglaze with wine or tomato without worrying about reactive
  metal.
