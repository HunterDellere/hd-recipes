about: |
  The shape is the tool. A high-walled, round- or flat-bottomed pan concentrates heat at the base for rapid Maillard
  contact, then reroutes ingredients up the cooler walls between bursts of searing — that vertical movement is what
  gives a stir-fry its texture, with proteins seared and aromatics on the edge of caramelization rather than steamed
  into uniform mush.

  Carbon steel is the canonical material. It heats fast, holds a seasoning, and becomes effectively non-stick with
  use. The seasoning is the surface — never soap-scrub a wok; rinse hot, dry on the burner, wipe a film of oil.

  Spec that matters: 14-inch carbon steel. Flat-bottom for Western induction and electric coil. Round-bottom needs
  a wok ring on a gas burner and is otherwise the better shape, because the walls do their geometry job all the way
  down.
