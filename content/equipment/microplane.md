about: |
  The geometry is the point. The teeth are stamped, not punched, so the cutting edges shave rather than tear. The
  output dissolves on contact with heat or acid in a way a box grater's output cannot — which is why a cacio e pepe
  sauce built with a microplane emulsifies smoothly while one built with coarse grate goes grainy.

  Spec that matters: the "fine" or "zester" model, ~21cm cutting surface. Coarser variants exist; they're less
  useful for cheese, citrus zest, garlic, and ginger — the cases where the dissolving texture is the entire point.
