about: |
  A scale weighs in grams. That's the whole pitch, and it's the entire reason hd-recipes uses grams as the canonical
  qty across the catalog. Cup-and-spoon measurement carries 20–30% error on flour and brown sugar alone, because
  compaction varies with humidity, scoop technique, and how recently the bag was opened. Grams don't care about any
  of that. A gram is a gram.

  Spec that matters: 0.1g resolution for spices, salt, and chemical leaveners — the places where 0.5g of a 2g
  measurement is a real percentage error. 1g resolution is fine for general baking and most cooking. Capacity to
  5kg covers everything short of stockpot-scale work. A tare button is non-negotiable; without it you're doing
  arithmetic that the scale should be doing for you.

  The kitchen consequence: recipes scale linearly. Doubling a 280g flour measurement is 560g, full stop. Doubling
  "2¼ cups" depends on whether you scoop the same way twice. That difference is why a recipe that worked last
  Tuesday breaks on Sunday.
