about: |
  A 12-inch (30cm) skillet with sloped sides. The width is the spec that matters — surface area drives evaporation
  rate, browning rate, and the elbow room you need to finish pasta in the pan without sloshing emulsion over the
  rim. A narrow pan crowds protein and aromatics; the moisture they release can't evaporate fast enough, the
  surface temperature drops, and you end up steaming food you meant to sear.

  Material is a function of what you cook in it. Stainless tri-ply is the all-purpose answer: it builds fond,
  deglazes cleanly, and survives sauce work without reacting. Carbon steel is the high-heat answer — faster
  response, better sear, requires seasoning maintenance. Both have the sloped sides that let you toss and roll
  food without a spatula.
