about: |
  Dark soy (lao chou / 老抽) is aged longer than light soy and finished with molasses or caramel. The result is thicker, less salty, and dramatically darker — the color of strong coffee.

  Use it for color (red-cooked pork, tea eggs, lo mein, hong shao anything) and a touch of caramelized depth. It is not a 1:1 swap for light soy — using dark soy as your primary seasoning will make a dish taste flat and look like used motor oil.

  A typical recipe uses 1–2 tbsp dark soy alongside 3–4 tbsp light soy. Dark for the look, light for the salt and umami.
