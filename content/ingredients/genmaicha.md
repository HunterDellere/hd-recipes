about: |
  Genmaicha (玄米茶) is sencha or bancha blended with hatcha (roasted, polished brown rice), in roughly 1:1 ratio by volume. A portion of the rice often pops during roasting into small white kernels; some blends add a pinch of matcha powder (matcha-iri genmaicha) for color and body. The roasted rice contributes a warm, popcorn-toast sweetness that rounds the bright, grassy bitterness of the underlying green tea and produces a brew with surprisingly low astringency.

  Historical origin: a frugal tea, originally a way for households to stretch expensive green tea with cheap roasted grain. The flavor profile turned out to be exceptional and it became a respected style on its own merits.

  Brewing is forgiving: 4 g per 240 ml at 80°C for 1 minute. Unlike most green teas, genmaicha tolerates slightly hotter water (up to 85°C) because the rice already adds round sweetness that buffers any tannin extracted from the leaf. Multiple steeps work, the second draws less rice flavor and more leaf character.

  Excellent cold-brewed: 6 g per 1 L cold water, 6 to 8 hours refrigerated, strain. The roasted rice notes come through cleaner cold than hot.

storage: 'Airtight tin, cool dark cupboard. 6 months once opened. The roasted rice stays fresh longer than the green tea component; old genmaicha loses the leaf brightness first and tastes mostly of toasted rice.'

substitutions:
  - for: 'genmaicha'
    use: 'sencha + a small pinch of toasted plain popcorn'
    note: 'Approximates the popped-rice note. Loses the integration that comes from brewing together.'
  - for: 'genmaicha'
    use: 'hojicha (roasted green tea)'
    note: 'Different profile entirely, hojicha is roasted leaf, not rice. But shares the warm toasted character and works in cold brews where genmaicha is unavailable.'
