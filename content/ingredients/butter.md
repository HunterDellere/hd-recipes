about: |
  ~80% fat, 16% water, 2% milk solids (lactose + caseins), trace salt. The milk solids are what brown when you make beurre noisette — they're caseins that hit Maillard at ~150°C and turn amber, releasing nutty aroma compounds (mainly furfurals and pyrazines).

  Use unsalted for cooking so you control salt independently. Salted butter is for toast.

  European-style butter (84%+ fat) browns slower because it has less water; it's worth using when the butter itself is the point (compound butter, brown butter, finishing).

storage: 'Refrigerator, wrapped. Freezes well for 6 months. Keep away from strong-smelling foods — butter absorbs odors aggressively.'
