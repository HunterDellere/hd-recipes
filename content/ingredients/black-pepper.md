about: |
  The heat in black pepper is piperine — fat-soluble, not water-soluble. This is why blooming pepper in oil or butter (as in cacio e pepe) extracts dramatically more flavor than just adding it to the cooking water.

  Aroma comes from volatile terpenes (limonene, pinene, sabinene). They dissipate within minutes of grinding, which is why pre-ground pepper is structurally inferior. Grind whenever you use it — not before.

  Tellicherry peppercorns are larger, riper, and more complex. Malabar are the standard. Both DOP. Cracked coarse delivers more bloom than fine grind because the larger surface area releases piperine more completely into hot fat.

storage: 'Whole peppercorns: airtight jar, dark cupboard, indefinite. Ground pepper: useless after a week. Buy whole; grind to order.'
