about: |
  Ají panca is the dried form of a deep-red Capsicum baccatum cultivar (the same species as ají amarillo, different variety). The dried pod is wine-red, wrinkled, 8 to 12 cm long, with a smoky-sweet aroma that reads more like a Mexican ancho or pasilla than like a fresh chili. Heat level is low (1,000 to 1,500 SHU, comparable to a poblano), making it a flavor chili rather than a heat chili.

  The paste form (rehydrated dried pods, blended with garlic and oil) is the standard commercial product outside Peru. Belmont and Inca's Food jar it at roughly 280 g per jar. The paste reads dark wine-red, smooth, and aromatic; the smoky-fruity character is the structural signature.

  Functional category: smoky chili base. Ají panca is the marinade chili for anticuchos (Peruvian beef heart skewers), the slow-cook base for adobo arequipeño, and the colorant-and-flavor chili for many braised stews (seco de res, carapulcra). It carries a smoky-fruity character that pairs especially well with grilled and roasted meats.

  The dried pods (sold whole at some Latin American groceries) can be rehydrated at home: stem, seed, tear into pieces, cover with hot water 20 minutes, blend with the soaking liquid plus garlic and oil to a smooth paste.

storage: |
  Refrigerated after opening, 3 to 4 months in the original jar. Freeze in 1 tbsp portions for 12 months. Whole dried pods: cool dry pantry in a sealed bag, 12 months; older pods get brittle and lose aromatic depth.
