about: |
  Firm tofu is soy milk coagulated with calcium sulfate (the traditional Chinese coagulant, gypsum) or magnesium chloride (nigari, the traditional Japanese coagulant), pressed lightly to expel some whey, and sold as roughly 14 oz / 400 g blocks at 70 to 80% water by weight. The structural difference between firm, extra-firm, and medium-firm is the degree of pressing: more pressing means lower water content, denser texture, more resistance to falling apart in a wok. American grocery brands typically label firm at ~75% water, extra-firm at ~70%, super-firm at ~63%; Chinese-style firm tofu (老豆腐, lao doufu) is closer to American extra-firm. Japanese-style firm tofu (momen) is closer to American medium-firm.

  Coagulant matters. Calcium sulfate produces a tofu that is faintly mineral, slightly sweet, and reads softer at any given firmness grade, the textbook texture for Chinese mapo tofu, dry-pot, and stuffed tofu. Magnesium chloride produces a tofu with a denser, springier texture and a faintly bitter mineral note that Japanese and Korean cooks prefer for grilled and silken applications. Glucono delta-lactone (GDL) is a third coagulant used almost exclusively for silken tofu (see ingredients/silken-tofu) and produces an extremely smooth, custard-like curd that is too delicate for any firm-tofu use case.

  Cooking behavior. Firm tofu is the workhorse of Chinese tofu cookery because it holds shape across the high-heat, high-impact methods that the cuisine favors. Cubed and pan-seared in oil for 4 to 5 minutes per side, the surface develops a chewy golden crust while the interior stays soft; this is the prep step before adding to mapo, dry-pot, or hot-and-sour soup. Cubed and deep-fried at 175°C / 350°F for 3 to 4 minutes, firm tofu develops a crisp shell and a hollow center that absorbs sauce on contact; this is the technique behind Hunan stinky-tofu, Cantonese braised tofu, and the cubes that float on top of Sichuan dry-pot. Sliced and braised in soy-aromatic broth for 20 to 30 minutes, firm tofu absorbs flavor deeply and the texture goes from springy to silky-soft; this is hong shao tofu and Korean dubu jorim. Crumbled into ma po tofu, firm tofu (rather than silken) is the Sichuan grandmother-tradition choice, it gives a chewier, more substantial bite than the silken-tofu Western adaptation.

  Pressing for structural recipes. For deep-frying, dry-pot, or any recipe where the tofu must hold cubes under aggressive cooking, additional pressing past the package state lifts the result by a wide margin. Wrap the block in a clean kitchen towel, set on a plate, lay a flat board on top, and weight with a 1 to 2 kg weight (a heavy can or a cast-iron skillet) for 30 to 45 minutes. The block will give up 50 to 100 g of water and the resulting texture stir-fries and deep-fries dramatically better. Skip the press for braised preparations where the tofu should absorb broth.

  Buying notes. Refrigerated tofu in water-filled tubs is the standard form. Look for tubs with clear water (cloudy water means the tofu is past prime), the latest expiration date you can find (tofu loses texture as it ages even unopened), and Chinese-brand calcium-sulfate-set tofu (Pulmuone Calcium-Sulfate Set, House Foods Premium, Hodo) for Chinese cooking applications. Shelf-stable aseptic-pack tofu (Mori-Nu silken, etc.) is acceptable for silken applications but not equivalent for firm-tofu use. Frozen tofu (intentionally frozen and thawed before use) develops a uniquely spongy, meat-like texture that absorbs sauce dramatically; this is "freeze-and-thaw tofu" used in some Korean and Sichuan preparations and is a different ingredient functionally, see notes on the recipe pages that call for it.

storage: |
  Unopened in the original tub: as marked, typically 2 to 3 weeks past purchase if refrigerated continuously below 4°C. Once opened, transfer the tofu to a sealed container, cover with fresh cold water, and refrigerate; change the water daily and use within 5 days. Pressed tofu (water-removed) keeps 3 days in a sealed container with no water. Do not freeze unintentionally, freezing changes the texture irreversibly into the spongy freeze-thaw form. Off-cues are sour smell, slimy surface, or pink/yellow patches; discard.
