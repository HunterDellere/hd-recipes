about: |
  Niter kibbeh is butter clarified slowly with a charge of aromatics: ginger, garlic, onion, fenugreek seed, cardamom, cinnamon, and often holy basil (besobela) and koseret leaf. The butter is melted, the aromatics simmered in the fat for 30 to 45 minutes at a temperature low enough that the milk solids brown without burning, the whole thing is strained, and the result is a clear, deep-gold, intensely aromatic fat that keeps at room temperature for months because the water has been driven off.

  The fat is not seasoning that gets added to a finished dish. It is the cooking medium itself, structural to every Ethiopian wat: onions are dry-sweated in a heavy pot first, then niter kibbeh is added to lock the aromatics into the fat, then berbere is bloomed in the spiced butter, then meat or legumes go in. The dish carries the fenugreek-and-cardamom signature of the niter kibbeh in every spoonful. Substituting plain ghee or regular butter produces a recognizably different dish; the aromatic infusion is the point.

  Make a batch and keep it on the counter. The water has been driven off, the milk solids removed, and the antioxidant spices stabilize the fat. A jar lasts 3 to 6 months at room temperature, longer in the refrigerator. The work is front-loaded; the payoff is months of Ethiopian cooking on the right fat.

storage: 'Airtight glass jar at cool room temperature for 3 months; refrigerated for 6 months; freezer for a year. The fat solidifies in the fridge and softens to scoopable at room temperature. Failure mode is oxidation, the fat goes from clean-aromatic to paint-crayon on the nose; if in doubt, smell.'
