about: |
  Most US all-purpose flour sits at 10–12% protein. King Arthur is on the higher end (~11.7%); Gold Medal is lower (~10.5%). That protein is what forms gluten when you hydrate it and work the dough — more protein means more chew, less means more tenderness.

  For cookies and quick breads where you don't want to develop much gluten, mix until the streaks just disappear and stop. Overmixing tightens the crumb.

  Weigh, don't scoop. A cup measured by dipping can vary 20% depending on how packed it is. 1 cup ≈ 125g if spooned and leveled.

storage: 'Airtight container, cool dry pantry. Six months at room temperature; longer in the freezer. Whole-wheat flour goes rancid faster — refrigerate it.'
