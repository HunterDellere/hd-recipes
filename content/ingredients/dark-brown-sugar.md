about: |
  Dark brown sugar is the same construction as light — refined sucrose plus molasses — but with roughly twice the molasses, ~6.5% by weight. Everything that molasses does to a baked good, it does more of here.

  More water means more steam and more spread, especially in cookies. More acid (the molasses sits around pH 5) weakens gluten further and pushes Maillard harder, so crust color deepens noticeably and the toffee-bitter edge of caramelization shows up earlier. The molasses flavor itself moves from background to foreground — closer to gingerbread or sticky toffee than to plain "caramel."

  This is why dark brown is the default in chocolate-chip cookies for people who want a chewier, darker, more complex result, and in anything where molasses character is the point: gingerbread, sticky toffee pudding, BBQ rubs and sauces. It's not the right call in a cake where you want neutral sweetness — it'll color the crumb and shout over delicate flavors.

  Swap math is the practical part. Substituting 1:1 with light brown will read as "more flavor, slightly more spread" — usually fine, sometimes too much. Substituting 1:1 with granulated loses the molasses entirely; to replicate, add ~1 tbsp (20g) molasses per cup (213g) of granulated and reduce another liquid by the same amount.

storage: 'Airtight container at room temperature. Failure mode is hardening — the higher molasses load means dark brown actually holds moisture better than light brown, but once dried out it sets even harder. Revive with a slice of bread or damp paper towel sealed in the container overnight.'

substitutions:
  - for: 'dark brown sugar'
    use: 'light brown sugar'
    note: '1:1 by weight. Cookie spreads less, browns less, and tastes noticeably milder. Acceptable in most recipes; missed in gingerbread or anything where molasses is featured.'
  - for: 'dark brown sugar'
    use: 'granulated sugar + molasses'
    note: 'For 1 cup (213g): 193g granulated + 20g (~1 tbsp) molasses. Reduce other liquid by ~15g to compensate for the molasses water. Closest possible match.'
  - for: 'dark brown sugar'
    use: 'muscovado'
    note: 'Muscovado is genuinely unrefined — molasses is integral, not sprayed on. Slightly more moisture and a more complex, mineral-edged flavor. 1:1 by weight; expect a subtle upgrade.'
