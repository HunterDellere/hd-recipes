about: |
  Hard sheep's-milk cheese aged 5–8 months. Saltier and more aggressive than parmigiano (which is cow's milk). The lactic tang and salt level are why it's the canonical cheese for cacio e pepe and amatriciana — the salt does double duty as seasoning, and the sharp lactic note balances the fat.

  Look for "Pecorino Romano DOP" on the rind. Anything labeled just "pecorino" without DOP may be aged less, or made from goat or cow milk blends — usable but not the same.

seasonality: 'Year-round; lots from winter milk (after the ewes have been on hay) tend to be richer in fat.'
storage: 'Wrap in parchment, then loose foil, in the cheese drawer. Lasts 3–4 weeks. Never plastic — it sweats and gets soapy.'

substitutions:

    note: 'Lower salt, less sharp, more nutty. Add ~10% more salt to the dish to compensate.'
  - for: 'pecorino romano'
    use: 'aged manchego'
    note: 'Sheep''s milk and high salt — closest sub if you cannot find pecorino. Slightly sweeter.'
